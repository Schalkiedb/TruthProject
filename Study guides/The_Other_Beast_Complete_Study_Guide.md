## Introduction: The Second Beast Emerges

In Revelation 13, we encounter two beasts. The first represents papal Rome, rising from the sea (populated Europe) and ruling for 1,260 years (538-1798 CE). *(This power shares key characteristics with the Little Horn of Daniel 7, studied in a companion guide — both prophecies describe the same papal system from different prophetic angles.)* But just as that power receives its deadly wound in 1798, a second beast emerges—this time from the earth (sparsely populated region). This beast appears harmless, even Christ-like, yet ultimately speaks as a dragon and enforces worship of the first beast.

**This is one of the most sobering prophecies in Scripture**: A nation founded on Christian principles and civil/religious liberty will eventually betray those very foundations and become the enforcer of the mark of the beast.

**Foundation Principle**: Appearances deceive. A power can look Christian externally while operating with satanic authority internally.

**Study Methodology**: Let Scripture define the symbols, identify the power through prophetic characteristics, and understand the warning for God's people today.

### Revelation 13:11-17 - The Complete Description

**Revelation 13:16-17**: "And he causeth all, both small and great, rich and poor, free and bond, to **receive a mark in their right hand, or in their foreheads**: And that **no man might buy or sell**, save he that had the mark, or the name of the beast, or the number of his name."

## Part 2: Identifying Mark #1 - Rises from the Earth

### Biblical Symbolism: Sea vs. Earth

**Revelation 13:1**: "And I stood upon the sand of the **sea**, and saw a beast rise up **out of the sea**..."

**First Beast** = rises from **sea**

**Revelation 13:11**: "And I beheld another beast **coming up out of the earth**..."

**Second Beast** = rises from **earth**

**What does "sea" represent?**

**Revelation 17:15**: "And he saith unto me, The **waters** which thou sawest, where the whore sitteth, are **peoples, and multitudes, and nations, and tongues**."

**Biblical Symbol Established**:
- **Sea/Waters** = densely populated areas, many nations/peoples (Revelation 17:15 — directly stated)
- **Earth** (by contrast) = sparsely populated area, wilderness, relatively unpopulated region *(deduced by contrast with the established sea symbol — a standard interpretive principle in prophetic hermeneutics)*

**Application**: The first beast (papal Rome) arose from the populated regions of Europe—amid the divisions of the Roman Empire, surrounded by established nations.

**The second beast arises in a relatively unpopulated area of the world**—not from among established kingdoms, but in a wilderness region, a "new world."

### Historical Context

**1798 CE** (when first beast receives deadly wound) - What major nation was emerging in a sparsely populated region of the world?

**Answer**: The United States of America

- Founded in wilderness territory (from European perspective)
- Native populations sparse compared to Europe
- Proclaimed independence 1776
- Constitution ratified 1788
- Rising to prominence as papal power was wounded (1798)

**Identifying Mark #1 Fulfilled**: Rises from earth (New World, Americas) rather than sea (Old World, Europe).

## Part 4: Identifying Mark #3 - Rises Slowly (No Wings)

### Symbolism of Wings in Prophecy

**Daniel 7:4**: "The first was like a **lion**, and had **eagle's wings**..."

**Symbol**: **Wings** = rapid conquest, swift rise to power

**Daniel 7:6**: "...a **leopard**, ...upon the back of it **four wings of a fowl**..."

**Greece** (leopard with four wings) = Alexander the Great conquered the known world in just 10 years—the most rapid conquest in ancient history. Four wings = extraordinary speed.

**Revelation 13:11**: "And I beheld another beast coming up out of the earth; and he had two horns like a lamb..."

**Notice**: **No wings mentioned**

**Significance**: This power rises **gradually, peacefully, without rapid military conquest**—growing slowly over time rather than through swift warfare. *(Note: The absence of wings is one element of the picture; the gradual rise conclusion is confirmed by combining this detail with the historical record of America's development — Scripture and history interpret each other.)*

**1776-1800**: Small nation of 13 colonies, population ~5 million
**1800-1850**: Westward expansion, gradual growth
**1850-1900**: Industrialization, increasing influence
**1900-1945**: Emerging world power
**1945-Present**: Global superpower

**No wings** = gradual, steady rise over 150+ years, not rapid conquest like previous empires.

**Identifying Mark #3 Fulfilled**: Rises slowly and peacefully to power, without the rapid military conquest symbolized by wings.

## Part 7: Identifying Mark #6 - Two Horns (Separation of Church and State)

**Two Distinct Realms**:

- Democratic principles
- Civil rights and freedoms
- Constitutional protections

**Second Horn**: **Protestantism/Religious Liberty**
