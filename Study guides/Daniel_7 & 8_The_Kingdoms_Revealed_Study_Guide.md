# Daniel 7 & 8: The Kingdoms Revealed
## A Complete Bible Study Guide

**The Same Kingdoms from a Different Perspective**

> **How this guide uses Daniel 7 and Daniel 8 together**: **Daniel 7** is the primary prophetic vision studied here — the four beasts, the little horn, and the heavenly judgment. **Daniel 8** is used as a *direct biblical identifier* within this guide: God Himself names Medo-Persia (Daniel 8:20) and Greece (Daniel 8:21-22), confirming the identity of the bear and the leopard with certainty. This is the Bible interpreting itself. Daniel 8 also introduces the 2,300-day prophecy and expands on the little horn — those elements are studied in the companion guide on Daniel 8.

---

## Introduction: Why Three Prophecies About the Same Kingdoms?

God did not give Daniel just one prophecy about world history — He gave **three overlapping prophecies**, each adding layers of detail:

| | **Daniel 2** | **Daniel 7** | **Daniel 8** |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Seen by** | Nebuchadnezzar (pagan king) | Daniel (God's prophet) | Daniel (God's prophet) |
| **Symbols** | Metallic image | Four beasts | Ram and goat |
| **Kingdoms** | Babylon, Medo-Persia, Greece, Rome | Babylon, Medo-Persia, Greece, Rome | Medo-Persia and Greece (named directly) |
| **Focus** | The succession of empires | The little horn and heavenly judgment | The little horn's activities in greater detail |
| **Perspective** | Human glory | God's view of kingdoms | God's sanctuary and the 2,300 days |
| **Identifies** | What | Who (beasts) | **Names them directly** (Daniel 8:20-21) |

**This guide focuses on Daniel 7 as the primary text, and uses Daniel 8 as a biblical cross-reference** — because God Himself names Medo-Persia (Daniel 8:20) and Greece (Daniel 8:21) by name, removing all guesswork about the bear and the leopard.

**Two perspectives on the same kingdoms**:
1. **Human perspective** (Daniel 2): Kingdoms appear magnificent, powerful, glorious — precious metals
2. **God's perspective** (Daniel 7): Kingdoms are brutal, beastly, oppressive — savage animals

**Why study all three together?**

1. **Confirms the interpretation** — When three independent prophecies describe the same kingdoms, we know the interpretation is correct
2. **Adds crucial details** — Daniel 7 reveals the **little horn power** (not detailed in Daniel 2) that persecutes God's people; Daniel 8 names them and gives the 2,300-day timeline
3. **Shows God's viewpoint** — What humans admire, God despises; what humans glorify, God judges
4. **Reveals the judgment** — Daniel 7 shows the **heavenly courtroom** where God judges these beast kingdoms

**Key Principle**: We will again use **only the Bible** to interpret this prophecy. Scripture will interpret Scripture. No human wisdom—just God's Word.

## Part 1: The Setting - Daniel's Vision (553 BCE)

### When and Where Did This Vision Occur?

> **Daniel 7:1** - "In the first year of Belshazzar king of Babylon Daniel had a dream and visions of his head upon his bed: then he wrote the dream, and told the sum of the matters."

**Historical Context**:
- **Date**: First year of Belshazzar = approximately **553 BCE**
- **Timeline**: About **50 years after** Nebuchadnezzar's dream in Daniel 2 (603 BCE)
- **Location**: Babylon (still the dominant world power)
- **Who**: Daniel himself receives the vision (not interpreting someone else's dream this time)

**Important Note**: Daniel 7 occurred **before** Daniel 5 (Belshazzar's feast) and **before** Daniel 6 (Daniel in the lion's den). The chapters are arranged **thematically**, not chronologically.

---

**Why Belshazzar?**

Belshazzar was co-regent with his father Nabonidus. While Nabonidus was away campaigning, Belshazzar ruled Babylon. He was the **last king** before Babylon fell to Medo-Persia in 539 BCE (just 14 years after this vision).

**Significance**: God gave Daniel this vision while Babylon was still in power, showing that the first beast (lion) represented Babylon—confirming the interpretation before it even fell.

---

### Daniel's Response: He Wrote It Down

> **Daniel 7:1b** - "...then he wrote the dream, and told the sum of the matters."

**Why this matters**:
- Daniel **documented** the vision immediately
- He recorded "the sum of the matters" = main points, essential details
- This written record preserved God's prophecy for **over 2,500 years**
- We can read the exact same words Daniel wrote in 553 BCE

**Application**: When God reveals truth, write it down. Don't trust memory alone. Preserve what God shows you.

## Part 3: Beast #1 - The Lion (Babylon)

### Description of the First Beast

> **Daniel 7:4** - "The first was like a lion, and had eagle's wings: I beheld till the wings thereof were plucked, and it was lifted up from the earth, and made stand upon the feet as a man, and a man's heart was given to it."

**The Lion's Characteristics**:
1. **Like a lion** - King of beasts, fierce, powerful
2. **Eagle's wings** - Swift conquest, speed, majesty
3. **Wings plucked** - Loss of power, conquest slowed
4. **Made to stand as a man** - Humanized, weakened
5. **Man's heart given** - Lost its fierce, conquering nature

---

### Biblical Identification: Lion = Babylon

How do we know the lion represents Babylon? **The Bible tells us directly**.

> **Jeremiah 4:7** - "The lion is come up from his thicket, and the destroyer of the Gentiles is on his way; he is gone forth from his place to make thy land desolate; and thy cities shall be laid waste, without an inhabitant."

**Context**: This is Jeremiah's prophecy about **Babylon** invading Judah. God calls Babylon "the lion."

---

> **Jeremiah 50:17** - "Israel is a scattered sheep; the lions have driven him away: first the king of Assyria hath devoured him; and last this Nebuchadrezzar king of Babylon hath broken his bones."

**Direct identification**: 
- Israel = scattered sheep
- **Lions** = Assyria and **Babylon** (Nebuchadnezzar specifically named)

**Confirmed**: The lion = Babylon under Nebuchadnezzar.

---

### The Eagle's Wings: Swift Conquest

> **Jeremiah 4:13** - "Behold, he shall come up as clouds, and his chariots shall be as a whirlwind: his horses are swifter than eagles. Woe unto us! for we are spoiled."

**Description of Babylon's military**:
- "Swift as eagles" = rapid conquest
- "Chariots as a whirlwind" = overwhelming force
- "Come up as clouds" = massive army

---

> **Deuteronomy 28:49** - "The LORD will bring a nation against you from afar, from the end of the earth, as swift as the eagle flies, a nation whose language you will not understand"

**Prophecy of invading nation**: Described as swift as an eagle (fulfilled by Babylon).

---

**Why horses/chariots symbolize military power**:

> **Deuteronomy 20:1** - "When thou goest out to battle against thine enemies, and seest horses, and chariots..."

> **Revelation 19:11** - "And I saw heaven opened, and behold a white horse; and He that sat upon him was called Faithful and True, and in righteousness he doth judge and make war."

**Horses and chariots** = military might, warfare, conquest

**Application**: The **lion with eagle's wings** perfectly describes Babylon's swift, powerful military conquests under Nebuchadnezzar.

---

### Wings Plucked: Babylon's Decline

> **Daniel 7:4b** - "...the wings thereof were plucked, and it was lifted up from the earth, and made stand upon the feet as a man, and a man's heart was given to it."

**What happened?**

After Nebuchadnezzar's pride led to his humiliation (Daniel 4), Babylon **lost its conquering nature**:

1. **Wings plucked** = No more rapid expansion
2. **Made to stand as a man** = Became weak, human, vulnerable
3. **Man's heart given** = Lost its fierce, lion-like nature

**Historical fulfillment**: 
- Nebuchadnezzar's successors were weak kings
- Babylon stopped expanding militarily
- By Belshazzar's reign (Daniel 5), Babylon was corrupt, decadent, ready to fall
- In 539 BCE, Medo-Persia conquered Babylon **without a fight** (Cyrus diverted the Euphrates and entered through the riverbed)

**The fierce lion had become a weak man.**

## Part 4: Beast #2 - The Bear (Medo-Persia)

### Description of the Second Beast

> **Daniel 7:5** - "And behold another beast, a second, like to a bear, and it raised up itself on one side, and it had three ribs in the mouth of it between the teeth of it: and they said thus unto it, Arise, devour much flesh."

**The Bear's Characteristics**:
1. **Like a bear** - Powerful, crushing, brutal
2. **Raised up on one side** - Lopsided, one part stronger than the other
3. **Three ribs in its mouth** - Three conquered territories
4. **"Arise, devour much flesh"** - Command to conquer more

---

### Biblical Identification: Bear = Medo-Persia

How do we know? **Daniel 8 directly identifies this kingdom.**

> **Daniel 8:3-4** - "Then I lifted up mine eyes, and saw, and, behold, there stood before the river a ram which had two horns: and the two horns were high; but one was higher than the other, and the higher came up last. I saw the ram pushing westward, and northward, and southward; so that no beasts might stand before him, neither was there any that could deliver out of his hand; but he did according to his will, and became great."

**Key details**:
- **Ram with two horns** = Two-part kingdom
- **One horn higher than the other** = One part more powerful
- **Higher came up last** = The stronger part rose second
- **Pushing west, north, south** = Three-directional conquest

---

> **Daniel 8:20** - "The ram which thou sawest having two horns are the kings of Media and Persia."

**God Himself identifies the ram = Media and Persia (Medo-Persia)**

**Connection to Daniel 7:5**:
- **"Raised up on one side"** = One horn higher = **Persia was stronger than Media**
- The Medes conquered Babylon first, but the Persians (under Cyrus) became dominant
- **"One horn higher, came up last"** = Persia rose to power after Media, became the dominant part

---

### The Three Ribs: Three Major Conquests

> **Daniel 7:5b** - "...it had three ribs in the mouth of it between the teeth of it..."

> **Daniel 8:4** - "I saw the ram pushing westward, and northward, and southward..."

**The three ribs represent the three major kingdoms Medo-Persia conquered**:

1. **Lydia** (West) - Conquered by Cyrus in 547 BCE
   - Located in western Asia Minor (modern Turkey)
   - Wealthy kingdom famous for gold (King Croesus)
   
2. **Babylon** (North) - Conquered by Cyrus in 539 BCE
   - The previous world empire
   - Conquered when Cyrus diverted the Euphrates River
   
3. **Egypt** (South) - Conquered by Cambyses II (Cyrus's son) in 525 BCE
   - Ancient civilization, long-standing power
   - Last major independent kingdom in the region

**The "three ribs"** = Lydia, Babylon, Egypt—the three great powers Medo-Persia crushed.

- "Devoured much flesh" = conquered and absorbed many nations

**The bear represents brute strength**—not the swift elegance of the lion/eagle (Babylon), but crushing, overwhelming force.

### The Four Wings: Unmatched Speed of Conquest

> **Daniel 7:6a** - "...like a leopard, which had upon the back of it four wings of a fowl..."

**Why four wings?**

**Comparison**:
- Babylon (lion) = **two wings** (eagle's wings = two) = swift conquest
- Greece (leopard) = **four wings** = **twice as fast** = conquered world in just **13 years**!

**The key point**: An eagle has two wings. The leopard had *double* that—four wings on an already fast animal emphasize extraordinary speed. Greece under Alexander moved faster than any empire before it.

**Historical fact**: **Alexander the Great** (336-323 BCE) conquered the known world faster than any conqueror before or since:

**Timeline of Alexander's Conquests**:
- **334 BCE** - Invaded Persia, won Battle of Granicus
- **333 BCE** - Won Battle of Issus, defeated Persian King Darius III
- **332 BCE** - Conquered Egypt, founded Alexandria
- **331 BCE** - Won Battle of Gaugamela, destroyed Persian Empire
- **330 BCE** - Captured Persian capital Persepolis
- **327 BCE** - Reached India
- **323 BCE** - Died in Babylon at age 32

**In just 13 years** (ages 20-32), Alexander conquered:
- Greece
- Asia Minor (Turkey)
- Syria
- Egypt
- Entire Persian Empire
- Parts of India

**"Touched not the ground"** (Daniel 8:5) = moved so fast his feet barely touched earth—perfect description of Alexander's lightning campaigns.

**The four wings represent unmatched speed.**

### The Four Heads: Division After Alexander's Death

> **Daniel 7:6b** - "...the beast had also four heads..."

> **Daniel 8:8** - "Therefore the he goat waxed very great: and when he was strong, the great horn was broken; and for it came up four notable ones toward the four winds of heaven."

> **Daniel 8:22** - "Now that being broken, whereas four stood up for it, four kingdoms shall stand up out of the nation, but not in his power."

**Historical fulfillment**:

When Alexander died suddenly in 323 BCE (age 32), his empire was divided among **four of his generals**:

1. **Cassander** - Took **Greece** and Macedonia
2. **Lysimachus** - Took **Asia Minor** (Turkey)
3. **Seleucus** - Took **Syria** and the eastern territories
4. **Ptolemy** - Took **Egypt**

**"Four kingdoms... but not in his power"** = None of Alexander's four successors had his strength or unified control. The empire was permanently divided.

**The four heads perfectly fulfill the prophecy.**

### "Dominion Was Given To It"

> **Daniel 7:6c** - "...and dominion was given to it."

**Key phrase**: "**was given**" = passive voice = God gave Greece dominion over the world

**This wasn't luck or mere military skill—it was divine appointment.**

**Historical evidence of divine providence**:

When Alexander reached Jerusalem, he planned to destroy it for refusing to help him during his siege of Tyre. But something remarkable happened:

**Alexander the Great's own testimony** (recorded by Jewish historian Josephus):

> "I did not adore him, but that God who hath honored him with his high-priesthood. For I saw this very person in a dream, in this very habit, when I was at Dios in Macedonia, who, when I was considering with myself how I might obtain the dominion of Asia, exhorted me to make no delay, but boldly to pass over the sea thither, for that he would conduct my army and would give me the dominion over the Persians; whence it is that having seen no other in that habit, and now seeing this person in it, and remembering that vision, and the exhortation which I had in my dream, **I believe that I bring this army under the Divine conduct, and shall therewith conquer Darius, and destroy the Power of the Persians, and that all things will succeed according to what is in my mind.**" - Alexander the Great (Josephus, *Antiquities of the Jews*, book xi, chapter 8, par. 5)

**Alexander admitted**:
1. He had a dream from God
2. God told him to conquer Asia
3. God promised to give him dominion over the Persians
4. He believed he conquered **under Divine conduct**

**Even pagan Alexander acknowledged God gave him dominion!**

## Part 6: Beast #4 - The Terrible Beast (Rome)

### Description of the Fourth Beast

> **Daniel 7:7** - "After this I saw in the night visions, and behold a fourth beast, dreadful and terrible, and strong exceedingly; and it had great iron teeth: it devoured and brake in pieces, and stamped the residue with the feet of it: and it was diverse from all the beasts that were before it; and it had ten horns."

**The Fourth Beast's Characteristics**:

1. **Dreadful and terrible** - Inspires fear and horror
2. **Strong exceedingly** - Most powerful of all beasts
3. **Great iron teeth** - Crushes everything (note: IRON, matching Daniel 2!)
4. **Devoured and brake in pieces** - Total destruction of enemies
5. **Stamped the residue** - Whatever it didn't eat, it trampled
6. **Diverse from all the beasts** - Unlike any before it
7. **Had ten horns** - Divided into ten parts

---

### Biblical Identification: The Terrible Beast = Rome

**How do we know?** Historical succession and biblical clues.

**Connection to Daniel 2**:

> **Daniel 2:40** - "And the fourth kingdom shall be strong as iron: forasmuch as iron breaketh in pieces and subdueth all things: and as iron that breaketh all these, shall it break in pieces and bruise."

**Key word**: **IRON**

> **Daniel 7:7** - "...and it had great iron teeth..."

**Same word**: **IRON**

**Both prophecies describe the fourth kingdom with IRON**:
- Daniel 2: Iron legs
- Daniel 7: Iron teeth

**Confirmed**: Fourth beast = Fourth kingdom = **Rome**

---

### Why Rome Is "Diverse From All the Beasts"

> **Daniel 7:7b** - "...and it was diverse from all the beasts that were before it..."

**How was Rome different?**

**1. Form of Government**:
- Babylon = Absolute monarchy (one king rules all)
- Medo-Persia = Dual monarchy (Medes and Persians)
- Greece = Divided monarchy (four kingdoms)
- **Rome** = **Republic, then Empire** (Senate, consuls, then emperors—unique governmental system)

**2. Legal System**:
- Rome developed **Roman law**—foundation of modern Western legal systems
- Concept of citizenship, legal rights, contracts
- **Most influential legal system in human history**

**3. Military Organization**:
- Rome's **legions** were unmatched in discipline, training, and strategy
- Professional standing army (not just conscripts)
- Superior engineering (roads, siege weapons, fortifications)

**4. Duration**:
- Rome lasted longer than any previous empire:
  * Western Roman Empire: 168 BCE - 476 CE (644+ years)
  * Eastern Roman Empire (Byzantine): Continued until 1453 CE (1,621 years total!)

**5. Cultural Impact**:
- Latin language influenced all Romance languages (Spanish, French, Italian, Portuguese, Romanian)
- Roman architecture, art, literature shaped Western civilization
- Rome ruled during time of Christ—crucified Jesus under Roman authority

**6. Religious Role**:
- Rome transformed from pagan empire to vehicle for **papal power** (the little horn, which we'll study next)
- Only empire that continued through religious transformation

**Rome was diverse—unlike anything before it.**

### The Ten Divisions of Rome (476 CE)

When Western Rome fell in **476 CE**, it divided into **ten major Germanic tribes**:

**The Ten Horns (Ten Germanic Tribes)**:

1. **Franks** → Modern **France**
2. **Anglo-Saxons** → Modern **England**
3. **Alemanni** → Modern **Germany**
4. **Suevi** → Modern **Portugal**
5. **Visigoths** → Modern **Spain**
6. **Lombards** → Modern **Italy**
7. **Burgundians** → Modern **Switzerland**
8. **Heruli** → **Destroyed 493 CE**
9. **Vandals** → **Destroyed 534 CE**
10. **Ostrogoths** → **Destroyed 538 CE**

**Note**: Three tribes (Heruli, Vandals, Ostrogoths) were destroyed. We'll see why in the next section (the little horn).

**Seven remain as modern nations**: France, England, Germany, Portugal, Spain, Italy, Switzerland

**Exact fulfillment**: Western Rome divided into exactly **ten** parts, just as prophesied.

**The Little Horn's Characteristics**:

1. **"Came up among them"** - Rose among the ten horns (tribes of Europe)
2. **"Another" little horn** - Different from the ten
3. **Started small** ("little") - Began with little power
4. **Three horns plucked up by the roots** - Destroyed three of the ten tribes
5. **"Eyes like the eyes of man"** - Intelligence, shrewdness, human wisdom
6. **"Mouth speaking great things"** - Makes proud, blasphemous claims

**This little horn is the most important part of Daniel 7**—it represents a power that persecutes God's people and continues until Christ's return.

---

### The Three Horns Plucked Up by the Roots

> **Daniel 7:8b** - "...before whom there were three of the first horns plucked up by the roots..."

**Historical fulfillment**: Three of the ten Germanic tribes were **destroyed** in the 5th-6th centuries:

---

**1. The Heruli (Destroyed 493 CE)**

> **Daniel 7:8** - "...there came up among them another little horn, before whom there were three of the first horns plucked up by the roots..."

**Historical fact**: Emperor Zeno (Eastern Roman Emperor) had commissioned **Theodoric the Ostrogoth** to march against the Heruli kingdom in Italy before Zeno's death in 491 CE. Theodoric completed this campaign in **493 CE**, destroying the Heruli kingdom under **Odoacer**.

**Result**: The Heruli kingdom was completely overthrown—"plucked up by the roots."

**Why were they destroyed?** The Heruli under Odoacer had resisted the bishop of Rome's growing claims to supreme authority. They were **Arian Christians** (who rejected the Trinitarian doctrine that Rome had made foundational to its authority).

---

**2. The Vandals (Destroyed 534 CE)**

**Historical fact**: Emperor Justinian I (Eastern Roman Emperor) sent his general **Belisarius** to conquer the **Vandals** in North Africa.

**Timeline**:
- **533 CE** - Belisarius landed in North Africa
- **534 CE** - Vandals completely defeated and their kingdom destroyed

**Why were they destroyed?** The Vandals were also **Arian Christians** who opposed papal authority and Catholic doctrine.

---

**3. The Ostrogoths (Destroyed 538 CE)**

**Historical fact**: After destroying the Vandals, Belisarius turned his attention to the **Ostrogoths** who controlled Rome itself.

**Timeline**:
- **536 CE** - Belisarius began siege of Rome
- **March 538 CE** - Belisarius drove the Ostrogoths out of Rome permanently

**Why were they destroyed?** The Ostrogoths were **Arian Christians** who had control over the city of Rome, preventing the bishop of Rome (pope) from exercising full power.

**Significance of 538 CE**: Once the Ostrogoths were removed from Rome, the papacy was free to exercise **supreme religious and political authority** in Western Europe. This date marks the beginning of **papal supremacy**—the 1,260 years of persecution prophesied in Daniel and Revelation.

---

### Summary: Why Three Tribes Were Destroyed

**All three tribes** (Heruli, Vandals, Ostrogoths) shared one thing in common:

**They opposed the papacy's doctrine and authority.**

- They were **Arian Christians** (rejected Trinity doctrine)
- They opposed the bishop of Rome's claim to supreme authority
- They stood in the way of papal power

**The Eastern Roman Empire** (under Zeno and Justinian), **allied with the papacy**, systematically destroyed these three tribes to clear the way for **papal supremacy**.

**"Plucked up by the roots"** = Completely destroyed, never rose again

**Exact fulfillment**: Three horns destroyed, seven remained (France, England, Germany, Portugal, Spain, Italy, Switzerland)—exactly as prophesied.

## Part 9: The Judgment Scene - God's Courtroom

**The Heavenly Courtroom**:

**Who is present?**

2. **"Thousand thousands ministered unto Him"** = 1,000,000 angels serving
3. **"Ten thousand times ten thousand stood before Him"** = 100,000,000 angels as witnesses
4. **Thrones** (plural) = God and His divine council

**What happens?**
- **"Thrones were cast down"** = The Aramaic word (*remah*) is better rendered **"thrones were placed/set"**—judgment seats were arranged for the court. Many modern translations (NIV, ESV, NASB) render this as "thrones were set in place." The primary meaning is that the heavenly court convened, with thrones positioned for the divine council. The earthly thrones of beast kingdoms are **also** being brought into account before God's throne.

- **"The books were opened"** = Records of human lives examined

**Description of God**:
- **"Garment white as snow"** = Perfect purity, righteousness
