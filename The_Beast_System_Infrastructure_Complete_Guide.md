## Central Bank Digital Currencies (CBDCs): Programmable Money

### What Are CBDCs?

**Central Bank Digital Currencies (CBDCs)** are digital versions of national currencies issued and controlled directly by central banks (not commercial banks or cryptocurrencies like Bitcoin).

**Key Characteristics:**
- **Centrally controlled** - Government has direct oversight
- **Programmable** - Money can have rules attached
- **Traceable** - Every transaction recorded permanently
- **Instant settlement** - No intermediaries needed
- **Digital only** - No physical cash equivalent

**Examples:**
- China's **Digital Yuan (e-CNY)** - Operational since 2020
- European Union's **Digital Euro** - In development (pilot phase 2024-2025)
- United States **Digital Dollar** - Under research (Federal Reserve)
- India's **Digital Rupee (e₹)** - Pilot launched 2022
- Nigeria's **eNaira** - Launched 2021
- Bahamas' **Sand Dollar** - First CBDC (2020)

**Status as of January 2026:**
- **130 countries** (representing 98% of global GDP) are exploring CBDCs
- **64 countries** are in advanced stages (development, pilot, or launch)
- **11 countries** have fully launched CBDCs
- **G20 nations** committed to CBDC interoperability by 2027

---

### How CBDCs Differ from Cash

| **Cash** | **CBDC** |
|----------|----------|
| Anonymous | Every transaction tracked |
| Peer-to-peer (no intermediary) | Central bank controls all transactions |
| Cannot be frozen or blocked | Can be frozen instantly |
| Cannot expire | Can have expiration dates |
| No spending restrictions | Can be programmed with restrictions |
| Works offline | Requires digital infrastructure |
| Physical possession = ownership | Government permission = access |
| Universal acceptance | Requires digital ID verification |

**Critical Difference:**

**Cash** - You own it. Once you have physical cash, no one can stop you from using it (unless physically prevented).

**CBDC** - You don't own it. You have **permission** to access it. That permission can be revoked instantly, automatically, and remotely.

---

### Sunday Blue Laws: The Legal Foundation Already Exists in America

**A critical fact that most Christians don't know: Sunday laws are not coming to America for the first time. They were already there. Many are still active.**

**Sunday laws (also called "Blue Laws") are laws that restrict commerce, labor, and certain activities on Sunday. America has had them since the colonial era.** The legal infrastructure for Sunday enforcement is not theoretical — it has precedent in every one of the 50 states.

---

#### The Colonial Origins

**The first Sunday law in America was passed in Virginia in 1610:**
> *"Every man and woman shall repair in the morning to the divine service and sermons preached upon the Sunday... and in the afternoon to divine service and catechizing... upon pain of losing their provision and allowance for the whole week following."*

Missing Sunday worship meant losing your food ration — economic exclusion for religious non-compliance in 1610 America. The first Sunday law enacted the exact mechanism Revelation 13:17 describes.

**All 13 original colonies had Sunday laws by the time of the Revolution.**

---

#### What Blue Laws Still Exist Today (2026)

**Federal Level:**
- No federal Sunday law currently, but **no constitutional barrier has been found**
- The Supreme Court explicitly **upheld Sunday laws as constitutional** in 1961 (*McGowan v. Maryland*, 366 U.S. 420)

**State Level — Still Active or Recently Active:**

|-------|------------------|
| **Pennsylvania** | Some Sunday trading restrictions remain; large appliance and automobile sales historically restricted |
| **New Jersey** | County-option Blue Laws remain; Bergen County restricts most retail on Sunday |

Bergen County retains one of the most comprehensive Blue Laws in the nation. Most retail stores **cannot open on Sundays**. This is enforced, with fines for violations. Bergen County residents have voted to keep the Sunday closing laws multiple times.

**Texas Car Dealerships:**

#### The Supreme Court: Sunday Laws Are Constitutional

**McGowan v. Maryland (1961):**
The U.S. Supreme Court unanimously upheld Sunday closing laws. Chief Justice Earl Warren wrote the majority opinion:

> *"The Court found that Sunday closing laws, despite their religious origin, had become secular in purpose. The State's police power includes the authority to establish a uniform day of rest for labor. Sunday closing laws are therefore valid exercises of the police power of a state..."*

**What This Means:**
- Sunday laws have been tested and upheld by the highest court in the land
- The argument that "freedom of religion protects against Sunday law" has already been **rejected by the Supreme Court**
- A Congress that passes Sunday law will be building on already-cleared constitutional ground
- No new constitutional framework is needed

**Braunfeld v. Brown (1961)** — same term:
- Orthodox Jewish merchants challenged Sunday laws arguing they violated their free exercise of religion (they already observed Saturday and couldn't afford to close two days)
- Supreme Court upheld Sunday laws even against this compelling religious liberty argument
- **Precedent:** Sunday law can burden Sabbath-keepers; no constitutional remedy available
