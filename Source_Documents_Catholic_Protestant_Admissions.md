## Catholic Church Sources (38 Statements)

### Print/Book Sources

These are primarily historical books, catechisms, and printed materials that may be available through libraries, used book dealers, or digital archives.

#### 1. **The Convert's Catechism of Catholic Doctrine** (Rev. Peter Geiermann, C.SS.R., 1910)
- **Type**: Official Catholic catechism with Church approval
- **Availability**: Historical book, possibly available through:
  - Catholic university libraries
  - Archive.org digital library
  - Used book dealers specializing in Catholic literature
- **Key Content**: Q&A format explicitly teaching Saturday is biblical Sabbath, Church changed it to Sunday by its own authority
- **Search Terms**: "Geiermann Convert's Catechism 1910"

#### 2. **The Question Box: Answers to Questions Received on Missions to Non-Catholics**
- **Type**: Catholic missionary handbook (Paulist Fathers, various editions throughout 20th century)
- **Availability**: Catholic archives, Paulist Fathers historical collections
- **Key Content**: Acknowledges biblical Sabbath is Saturday, Church transferred to Sunday by authority
- **Search Terms**: "Question Box Paulist Fathers Catholic missions"

#### 3. **Canon Cafferata, The Catechism Explained** (p. 89)
- **Type**: Standard Catholic catechism explanation
- **Availability**: Catholic theological libraries
- **Key Content**: "The Church substituted Sunday for Saturday by the plenitude of the divine power which she received from her Founder, Jesus Christ."

#### 4. **Rev. John Laux, M.A., A Course in Religion for Catholic High Schools and Academies** (Benzinger Brothers, 1936, Part 1, p. 51)
- **Type**: Catholic educational textbook
- **Availability**: Catholic school archives, used book dealers
- **Key Content**: Teaches God gave Church power to set aside holy days, Church chose Sunday

#### 5. **Vincent J. Kelly, Forbidden Sunday and Feast-Day Occupations** (Catholic University of America Press, 1943, Studies in Sacred Theology No. 70)
- **Type**: Academic theological dissertation
- **Availability**: 
  - Catholic University of America library archives
  - Theological academic libraries
- **Key Content**: "It was not until several hundred years after Christ that the Sabbath observance was by law transferred from Saturday to Sunday."

#### 6. **Henry Tuberville, An Abridgment of the Christian Doctrine** (1833 approbation, p. 58)
- **Type**: Historical Catholic doctrinal text
- **Availability**: Historical Catholic archives, rare book collections
- **Key Content**: Q&A proving Church has power to institute festivals, citing Sunday change as evidence
- **Note**: Also published as *Manual of Christian Doctrine*, ed. by Daniel Ferris (1916 ed.), p. 67

#### 7. **Adrien Nampon, Catholic Doctrine as Defined by the Council of Trent** (p. 157)
- **Type**: Explanation of Council of Trent doctrines
- **Availability**: Catholic theological libraries, historical archives
- **Key Content**: Acknowledges no biblical authorization for Sunday, states tradition supersedes Scripture

#### 8. **Library of Christian Doctrine: Why Don't You Keep Holy the Sabbath-Day?** (London: Burns and Oates, Ltd., pp. 3-4)
- **Type**: Catholic apologetic pamphlet
- **Availability**: British Catholic archives, Burns and Oates historical publications
- **Key Content**: Challenges Protestants on changing commandment without biblical authority

#### 9. **Monsignor Louis Segur, Plain Talk about the Protestantism of Today** (p. 213)
- **Type**: Catholic critique of Protestant inconsistency
- **Availability**: Catholic apologetics collections
- **Key Content**: "The observance of Sunday by the Protestants is an homage they pay, in spite of themselves, to the authority of the [Catholic] Church."

#### 10. **The Faith of Millions** (Rev. John A. O'Brien, PhD)
- **Type**: Popular Catholic apologetic work (widely distributed)
- **Availability**: 
  - Catholic bookstores (may still be in print)
  - Used book dealers
  - Library collections

#### 11. **James Cardinal Gibbons, The Faith of Our Fathers** (88th edition, p. 89; Originally 1876, republished 1980 by TAN Books)

- **Availability**:

### Protestant Periodical Sources

#### 17. **Dr. Edward T. Hiscox, paper read before New York ministers' conference** (Nov. 13, 1893)
- **Reported in**: *New York Examiner*, Nov. 16, 1893
- **Denomination**: Baptist (author of *The Baptist Manual*)
- **Type**: Address to ministers, reported in newspaper
- **Availability**: 
  - New York Examiner archives
  - Baptist historical society archives

## Archival Strategy

### High Priority - Web-Accessible Sources

These should be downloaded/saved first as they're readily available:

1. **Vatican Documents**:
   - Dies Domini (1998) - Full text available at vatican.va
   - Catholic Catechism - Available online at vatican.va
   - **Action**: Download PDF versions, save locally

2. **Protestant Historical Documents**:
   - Augsburg Confession - Multiple online versions available
   - John Wesley's Works - Online versions available
   - **Action**: Download from reliable theological websites

### Medium Priority - Archive.org and Digital Libraries

Check these sources for digitized historical books:

1. **Archive.org** (https://archive.org):
   - Search for historical Catholic catechisms (Geiermann, Keenan, Tuberville)
   - Search for Protestant theological works (Dwight, Dale, Neander)
   - Search for D.L. Moody's books
   - Search for Cardinal Gibbons' "Faith of Our Fathers"

2. **Google Books**:
   - Many older books available in full or preview
   - Search by author and title

3. **HathiTrust Digital Library** (https://www.hathitrust.org):
   - Large collection of digitized books from academic libraries
   - Good source for older theological works

### Lower Priority - Purchase/Library Access

For books still in print or available through libraries:

1. **Still in Print**:
   - Cardinal Gibbons, "Faith of Our Fathers" (TAN Books)
   - John Wesley's Works (various editions)
   - Catholic Catechism (current edition)

2. **Library Access**:
   - Interlibrary loan for historical books
   - University theological libraries (especially Catholic universities)
   - Denominational archives (Baptist, Methodist, Lutheran, etc.)

3. **Used Book Dealers**:
   - AbeBooks.com (specialty in rare/used books)
   - ThriftBooks.com
   - Catholic/Christian used bookstores

### Specialized Archives

#### Catholic Archives:
- **Baltimore Archdiocese Archives**: Cardinal Gibbons papers, official correspondence
- **Catholic University of America**: Theological dissertations, historical documents
- **Vatican Secret Archives**: (Access restricted, but some materials published)
- **Diocesan Archives**: Local parish bulletins, newspapers

#### Protestant Archives:
- **Yale University**: Timothy Dwight papers and sermons
- **Moody Bible Institute**: D.L. Moody materials
- **Lutheran Archives**: Augsburg Confession materials, denominational studies
- **Methodist Archives**: John Wesley works, denominational periodicals
- **Baptist Historical Societies**: Dr. Hiscox materials, Baptist periodicals

#### Newspaper Archives:
- **Newspapers.com**: Historical newspaper database (subscription)
- **Chronicling America** (Library of Congress): Free historical US newspaper database
- **State Historical Societies**: Local newspaper archives
- **Catholic Newspaper Archives**: Diocesan newspaper collections

## Download/Archive Instructions

### Step 1: Immediate Web Downloads (No Purchase Required)

**Priority downloads** - Start with these free, immediately accessible sources:

   - Dies Domini full text
   - Catechism sections on Ten Commandments and Sunday

   - John Wesley's Sermon 25 and other relevant sermons
   - Save as PDF

   - Create folder structure: "Catholic Sources" and "Protestant Sources"

### Step 3: Library Research

**For books not available online**:

1. **WorldCat** (https://www.worldcat.org):
   - Search for each book title

   - Request through interlibrary loan

2. **University Libraries**:
   - Catholic universities often have extensive Catholic historical collections
   - Seminary libraries (Catholic, Baptist, Methodist, Lutheran) have denominational materials

## Research Notes

### Success Likelihood by Source Type:

**Very High (90%+)**: 
- Vatican documents (Dies Domini, Catechism) - Fully available online
- Augsburg Confession - Fully available online
- John Wesley's works - Fully available online
- Archive.org digitized books - Many titles likely available

**High (60-90%)**:
- Google Books full-view titles (pre-1923 publications)
- HathiTrust academic library digitizations
- Historical newspaper databases (Chronicling America, Newspapers.com)

**Medium (30-60%)**:
- Historical Catholic periodicals (diocesan newspapers, Catholic magazines)
- Protestant denominational periodicals (Christian Advocate, etc.)
- Parish bulletins and local church publications

**Lower (10-30%)**:
- Personal correspondence (Cardinal Gibbons letters, etc.) - Require archive access
- Rare pamphlets and tracts - May require specialized collections
- Some out-of-print books not yet digitized

### Alternative If Physical Access Unavailable:

If you cannot access physical archives or certain materials:

1. **Citation Verification**: Most quotes from these sources have been verified and republished in multiple secondary sources. Cross-reference citations in:
   - Academic books on Sabbath history
   - Seventh-day Adventist historical documentation
   - Protestant Reformation history
   - Catholic apologetics literature

2. **Scholarly Databases**: University access to:
   - JSTOR (academic journal articles)
   - ProQuest (dissertations and historical documents)
   - Academic Search Complete
   - Religious and theological studies databases

3. **Interlibrary Loan**: Most university and public libraries participate in interlibrary loan systems allowing access to rare materials from other libraries nationwide.

## Estimated Costs

If purchasing materials rather than using free sources:

### Free Sources (Time Investment Only):
- Vatican documents: **Free**
- Archive.org downloads: **Free**
- Chronicling America: **Free**
- University library access (if affiliated): **Free**

### Paid Resources:

**Subscriptions** (consider temporary/trial):
- Newspapers.com: ~$10-20/month (often has free trial)
- Academic databases (JSTOR, ProQuest): Expensive individually, but free through university affiliation

**Book Purchases** (currently in print):
- Cardinal Gibbons, *Faith of Our Fathers* (TAN Books): ~$15-20
- John Wesley complete works: ~$30-50 (various editions)
- Catholic Catechism: ~$15-25

**Used Books** (out of print):
- Historical Catholic catechisms: $20-100 depending on rarity
- Protestant theological works: $15-75 depending on rarity
- Note: Prices vary widely; shop multiple sources

**Interlibrary Loan**:
- Usually **Free** or minimal fee ($5-15 per item)

**Photocopying/Scanning** (if visiting archives):
- Typically $0.10-0.25 per page
- Some archives charge research/access fees: $25-50

### Estimated Total Cost Range:

**Minimal Approach** (free sources only): **$0-50**
- Free downloads + one or two currently-in-print purchases

**Moderate Approach** (free + some purchases): **$100-300**
- Free downloads + several key books purchased + newspaper subscription (1 month)

**Comprehensive Approach** (all sources): **$500-1,000+**
- All free sources + book purchases + archive visits + professional researcher assistance

**Recommendation**: Start with all free sources first (likely to get 60-70% of materials), then assess which remaining sources are most critical before investing in purchases or travel to archives.
