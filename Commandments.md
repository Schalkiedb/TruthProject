# The Ten Commandments — Three-Way Comparison

> **Purpose**: A direct comparison of the Ten Commandments as given in Scripture (Exodus 20 & Deuteronomy 5), against the Roman Catholic Catechism version, and the Modern Protestant position — documenting where each tradition stands and what has been changed, removed, or reinterpreted.

---

## Why This Matters — The Prophetic Foundation

**Daniel 7:25** prophesied that a power arising after Rome would *"think to change times and laws."* The alterations to God's Ten Commandments documented in this file are not minor ecclesiastical adjustments — they are the direct historical fulfillment of that prophecy.

**Revelation 14:12** defines the final remnant people of God as those who *"keep the commandments of God and the faith of Jesus."* The question of which commandments — God's original ten, or a humanly revised version — is therefore not academic. It is the central issue of end-time worship.

**Revelation 13:16–17** warns of a coming "mark" enforced on all people. The Catholic Church itself calls Sunday observance its *"mark of authority"* (The Catholic Record, 1923). The connection between the altered 4th Commandment and the prophesied mark is made explicit in the admissions of Catholic sources below.

This document exists so that every reader can see, in plain language, what God commanded — and what was changed, by whom, and by what claimed authority.

## Summary of Changes

| **What Changed** | **Catholic** | **Modern Protestant** |
|-----------------|-------------|----------------------|
| **2nd Commandment** (Graven Images) | ❌ Removed — merged into 1st | ✅ Retained |
| **4th Commandment** (Sabbath Day) | ❌ Altered — changed to "Lord's Day" (Sunday); all reference to seventh day removed | ⚠️ Text sometimes kept, but **practice changed to Sunday** by tradition and catechism reinterpretation |
| **10th Commandment** (Coveting) | ❌ Split into two separate commandments | ✅ Retained as one |
| **Total Count** | Maintained at 10 by splitting the 10th | Maintained at 10 |
| **Authority for Changes** | Church Tradition / Papal Authority | Protestant Reformers adopted Sunday from Rome; no explicit biblical mandate given |

> ⚠️ **Lutheran Exception**: Martin Luther retained the Catholic/Augustinian commandment numbering — merging the 1st and 2nd commandments and splitting the 10th. Most Reformed, Baptist, Anglican, and Methodist traditions use the biblical numbering but still observe Sunday. Only Lutherans share the same altered structure as Catholics.

## Historical Timeline — How the Change Was Made

The alteration of the Sabbath commandment did not happen at one moment. It was a staged process using both civil and ecclesiastical authority:

| **Year** | **Event** | **Significance** |
|---------|-----------|------------------|
| **321 CE** | **Constantine's Sunday Edict** — *"On the venerable Day of the Sun let the magistrates and people residing in cities rest."* | First civil law enforcing Sunday rest. Made by a Roman emperor — not by Scripture, not by an apostle. |
| **325 CE** | **Council of Nicaea** | Separated Christian practices from Jewish ones; reinforced Sunday as the Christian day of assembly; Easter calculation moved away from Passover. |
| **363–364 CE** | **Council of Laodicea, Canon 29** — *"Christians must not judaize by resting on the Sabbath, but must work on that day... If any shall be found to be Judaizers, let them be anathema."* | Ecclesiastical ban on Saturday Sabbath observance. Christians who kept the biblical Sabbath were threatened with excommunication. |
| **1545–1563 CE** | **Council of Trent** | Formally defined Church tradition as equal in authority to Scripture; explicitly used Sunday observance as proof of that authority. |
| **1998 CE** | **Dies Domini** — Papal Apostolic Letter, Pope John Paul II | Called Sunday the *"distinguishing mark"* and *"sign of identity"* for Catholics. Declared Sunday Mass attendance "not a matter of choice" but a "duty." |

> **No biblical decree, apostolic command, or statement of Christ authorizes any of these changes.** The Bible is unanimous: *"The seventh day is the Sabbath of the LORD your God."* (Exodus 20:10)

## Commandment Numbering — The Shift That Conceals the Change

When the 2nd Commandment (graven images) is removed, every commandment that follows shifts up by one number. This means the Sabbath commandment — the most controversial change — **moves from its recognisable position as the "4th" to appear as the "3rd."** To a reader comparing lists without knowing what was removed, the deletion is invisible.

Splitting the 10th commandment into two restores the total count to ten, making the restructuring appear intact.

| **Bible #** | **Commandment** | **Catholic #** | **Effect of Renumbering** |
|-----------|---------------|--------------|---------------------------|
| 1st | No other gods | 1st | No change |
| **2nd** | **No graven images** | *(removed)* | **⬅️ Deleted entirely** |
| 3rd | No vain use of God's name | 2nd | Shifts up |
| **4th** | **Remember the Sabbath (seventh day)** | **3rd** | **⬅️ Renumbered AND reworded to "Lord's Day" (Sunday)** |
| 5th | Honour father and mother | 4th | Shifts up |
| 6th | No murder | 5th | Shifts up |
| 7th | No adultery | 6th | Shifts up |
| 8th | No stealing | 7th | Shifts up |
| 9th | No false witness | 8th | Shifts up |
| 10th | No coveting *(one commandment)* | 9th + 10th | **⬅️ Split into two to maintain the count of ten** |

> *"The very act of renumbering conceals the change to the Sabbath commandment."* — The Catholic numbering shift moves the Sabbath from its unmistakable 4th-commandment position and buries the alteration inside a renumbered list.

## The Key Alteration — The Sabbath Commandment in Full

**Bible (Exodus 20:8–11 — KJV):**
> *"Remember the sabbath day, to keep it holy. Six days shalt thou labour, and do all thy work: But the **seventh day** is the sabbath of the LORD thy God: in it thou shalt not do any work, thou, nor thy son, nor thy daughter, thy manservant, nor thy maidservant, nor thy cattle, nor thy stranger that is within thy gates: For in six days the LORD made heaven and earth, the sea, and all that in them is, and rested the seventh day: wherefore the LORD blessed the sabbath day, and hallowed it."*

**Catholic Catechism (Catechism of the Catholic Church, 3rd Commandment):**
> *"Remember to keep holy the Lord's Day."*

**Protestant (Westminster Shorter Catechism, Q59 — Presbyterian/Reformed):**
> *"God appointeth one day in seven to be kept holy unto him, which, from the beginning of the world to the resurrection of Christ, was the last day of the week; and, from the resurrection of Christ, was changed into the first day of the week, which in Scripture is called the Lord's day, and is to be continued to the end of the world, as the Christian Sabbath."*

> **Note:** The Bible contains no record of God, Christ, or the Apostles commanding or authorizing this change. The Resurrection occurred on the first day, but no commandment to thereafter worship on that day is recorded in Scripture.

## The Removal of the 2nd Commandment — What Was Taken Out

**Bible (Exodus 20:4–6 — KJV):**
> *"Thou shalt not make unto thee any graven image, or any likeness of any thing that is in heaven above, or that is in the earth beneath, or that is in the water under the earth. Thou shalt not bow down thyself to them, nor serve them: for I the LORD thy God am a jealous God, visiting the iniquity of the fathers upon the children unto the third and fourth generation of them that hate me; And shewing mercy unto thousands of them that love me, and keep my commandments."*

**Catholic Catechism:** This commandment does not appear as a numbered commandment. It is subsumed under the 1st Commandment.

**Protestant:** This commandment is retained but is often softened in practice. Many Protestant churches permit religious imagery while forbidding worship of it.

## Notable Admissions

### Catholic Admissions — In Their Own Words

**1. Cardinal Gibbons, Archbishop of Baltimore — Personal Signed Letter (1877–1921 tenure):**
> *"Is Saturday the seventh day according to the Bible and the Ten Commandments? I answer yes. Is Sunday the first day of the week and did the Church change the seventh day—Saturday—for Sunday, the first day? I answer yes. Did Christ change the day? **I answer no!**"*

**2. Cardinal Gibbons, *The Faith of Our Fathers*, 88th edition, pp. 72–73, 89:**
> *"You may read the Bible from Genesis to Revelation, and you will not find a single line authorizing the sanctification of Sunday. The Scriptures enforce the religious observance of Saturday, a day which we never sanctify."*
>
> *"Reason and sense demand the acceptance of one or the other of these alternatives: either Protestantism and the keeping holy of Saturday, or Catholicity and the keeping holy of Sunday. **Compromise is impossible.**"*

**3. The Convert's Catechism of Catholic Doctrine** — Rev. Peter Geiermann, C.Ss.R. (1910, Church-approved):
> *"Q: Which is the Sabbath day? A: Saturday is the Sabbath day.*
> *Q: Why do we observe Sunday instead of Saturday? A: We observe Sunday instead of Saturday because **the Catholic Church transferred the solemnity of Saturday to Sunday**.*
> *Q: By what authority did the Church substitute Sunday for Saturday? A: The Church substituted Sunday for Saturday by **the plenitude of that divine power which Jesus Christ bestowed upon her**."*

**4. The Catholic Mirror, September 1893** (Official Organ of Cardinal Gibbons):
> *"The Catholic Church, for over one thousand years before the existence of a Protestant, by virtue of her divine mission, changed the day from Saturday to Sunday... The Christian Sabbath is therefore to this day the acknowledged offspring of the Catholic Church as spouse of the Holy Ghost, without a word of remonstrance from the Protestant world."*

**5. T. Enright, C.S.S.R. — Public Lecture, 1893 (The $1,000 Challenge):**
> *"I have repeatedly offered $1,000 to anyone who can prove to me from the Bible alone that I am bound to keep Sunday holy. There is no such law in the Bible. It is a law of the holy Catholic Church alone. The Bible says, 'Remember the Sabbath day to keep it holy.' The Catholic Church says: 'No. By my divine power I abolish the Sabbath day and command you to keep holy the first day of the week.' And lo! The entire civilized world bows down in a reverent obedience to the command of the holy Catholic Church."*

**6. Saint Catherine Catholic Church Sentinel, May 21, 1995 (Pastor's Page):**
> *"Perhaps the boldest thing, the most revolutionary change the Church ever did, happened in the first century. The holy day, the Sabbath, was changed from Saturday to Sunday... **not from any directions noted in the Scriptures, but from the Church's sense of its own power**... People who think that the Scriptures should be the sole authority, should logically become 7th Day Adventists, and keep Saturday holy."*

**7. The Australian Catholic Truth Society Record, February 10, 1950:**
> *"Sunday is a Catholic institution and its claim to observance can be defended only on Catholic principles... **From beginning to end of Scripture there is not a single passage that warrants the transfer of weekly public worship from the last day of the week to the first.**"*

**8. Rev. John A. O'Brien PhD, *The Faith of Millions*:**
> *"Since Saturday, not Sunday, is specified in the Bible, isn't it curious that non-Catholics who profess to take their religion directly from the Bible and not from the Church, observe Sunday instead of Saturday?... this change was made about fifteen centuries before Protestantism was born, and by that time the custom was universally observed. They have continued the custom, even though it rests upon the authority of the Catholic Church and not upon an explicit text in the Bible. That observance remains as a reminder of the Mother Church from which the non-Catholic sects broke away — like a boy running away from home but still carrying in his pocket a picture of his mother or a lock of her hair."*

**9. Monsignor Louis Segur, *Plain Talk about the Protestantism of Today*, p. 213:**
> *"The observance of Sunday by the Protestants is an homage they pay, in spite of themselves, to the authority of the [Catholic] Church."*

**10. The Catholic Record** (London, Ontario — September 1, 1923):

> *"There was and is a commandment to keep holy the Sabbath day, but that Sabbath day was not Sunday... Not in the New Testament — absolutely not."*

**Methodist — Dr. R.W. Dale, *The Ten Commandments* (New York: Eaton & Mains, pp. 127–129):**

**Evangelist — D.L. Moody:**

**Methodist Founder — John Wesley, Sermon 25:**
> *"The moral law contained in the ten commandments, and enforced by the prophets, he [Christ] did not take away... This is a law which never can be broken... Every part of this law must remain in force upon all mankind, and in all ages."*

**Catholic Challenge to All Protestants — Priest Brady, Address reported in *Elizabeth, NJ News*, March 18, 1903:**
> *"It is well to remind the Presbyterians, Baptists, Methodists, and all other Christians, that the Bible does not support them anywhere in their observance of Sunday. Sunday is an institution of the Roman Catholic Church, and those who observe the day observe a commandment of the Catholic Church."*
