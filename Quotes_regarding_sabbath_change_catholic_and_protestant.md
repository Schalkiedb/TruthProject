### Catholic Church Sources

**Official Catholic Statements on Sunday Change**:

1. **The Catholic Mirror** (Official Organ of Cardinal Gibbons):
   - *"The Christian Sabbath, the Genuine Offspring of the Union of the Holy Spirit with the Catholic Church His Spouse"* - Series of articles, September 1893
   - **Key Quote**: "The Catholic Church, for over one thousand years before the existence of a Protestant, by virtue of her divine mission, changed the day from Saturday to Sunday... The Christian Sabbath is therefore to this day the acknowledged offspring of the Catholic Church as spouse of the Holy Ghost, without a word of remonstrance from the Protestant world."
   - **Significance**: Official Catholic publication explicitly claiming sole authority for Sabbath change

2. **Rome's Challenge: Why Do Protestants Keep Sunday?**:
   - Published by Catholic church sources (various editions, early-to-mid 1900s)
   - **Core Argument**: Challenges Protestants to justify Sunday observance when the Bible commands Saturday Sabbath
   - **Key Quote**: "The [Catholic] Church changed the observance of the Sabbath to Sunday by right of the divine, infallible authority given to her by her founder, Jesus Christ. The Protestant claiming the Bible to be the only guide of faith, has no warrant for observing Sunday."
   - **Significance**: Catholic Church openly challenges biblical authority of Sunday, claims only Catholic tradition supports it

3. **The Christian Sabbath** (Catholic Educational Literature):
   - Various Catholic publications explaining Sunday as "Christian Sabbath"
   - **Argument**: Church authority supersedes biblical commandment
   - **Position**: Catholics consistently acknowledge no biblical basis for Sunday, claim church authority alone made the change

4. **The Convert's Catechism of Catholic Doctrine** (Rev. Peter Geiermann, C.SS.R.):
   - Published 1910, approved by Catholic Church authorities
   - **Question and Answer Format**:
     - **Q**: "Which is the Sabbath day?"
     - **A**: "Saturday is the Sabbath day."
     - **Q**: "Why do we observe Sunday instead of Saturday?"
     - **A**: "We observe Sunday instead of Saturday because the Catholic Church, in the Council of Laodicea (A.D. 336), transferred the solemnity from Saturday to Sunday."
   - **Note**: The catechism incorrectly dates the Council of Laodicea to 336 CE; the actual date was approximately 363-364 CE. This historical error in the Catholic source does not change the admission that the Church, not Scripture, changed the Sabbath.
   - **Key Quote**: "The Church substituted Sunday for Saturday by the plenitude of that divine power which Jesus Christ bestowed upon her."
   - **Significance**: Official Catholic catechism for converts explicitly teaches that Saturday is the biblical Sabbath, but the Church changed it to Sunday by its own authority

5. **The Question Box: Answers to Questions Received on Missions to Non-Catholics**:
   - Catholic missionary handbook used for evangelism and answering Protestant objections
   - Published by Paulist Fathers (various editions throughout 20th century)
   - **On the Sabbath Question**:
     - Acknowledges the biblical Sabbath is Saturday
     - States Catholics transferred observance to Sunday by church authority
     - Presents the change as proof of Catholic Church's divine authority
   - **Key Argument**: "If Protestants follow the Bible only, they should observe Saturday; by keeping Sunday they acknowledge Catholic Church authority."
   - **Significance**: Used as evangelistic tool showing that Sunday observance proves Catholic Church has authority to change God's commandments - turns Protestant inconsistency into Catholic apologetic

6. **Saint Catherine Catholic Church Sentinel**, May 21, 1995 (Pastor's Page):

People who think that the Scriptures should be the sole authority, should logically become 7th Day Adventists, and keep Saturday holy."
   - **Significance**: Modern (1995) Catholic parish publication explicitly stating the Church changed the Sabbath "not from any directions noted in the Scriptures, but from the Church's sense of its own power" - remarkable candor that the change had no biblical basis and was based solely on claimed church authority

40. **The Roman Catechism (Catechism of the Council of Trent)**, promulgated by Pope Pius V (1566):

41. **Cardinal John Henry Newman, *An Essay on the Development of Christian Doctrine*** (1845; revised 1878):
   - Newman was the most celebrated English Catholic convert of the 19th century; this work is one of the most influential in Catholic apologetics
   - **Key Argument**: Newman argued that doctrines not explicitly found in Scripture (such as Sunday observance, purgatory, veneration of the saints) legitimately "develop" under the guidance of the Church's teaching authority over time
   - **On Tradition vs. Scripture**: "The sacred text was never intended to teach doctrine, but only to prove it; and if we would learn doctrine, we must have recourse to the formularies of the Church."
   - **On Sunday as Development**: Newman's "development of doctrine" framework is the precise Catholic theological argument used to explain how Saturday Sabbath became Sunday — not by the Bible, but by the living authority of the Church over time
   - **Significance**: Newman's framework is the most sophisticated Catholic explanation for Sunday observance — it openly admits the change has no scriptural basis but argues Church tradition is an equal (and superior) authority to Scripture

### Early Church History & Imperial Records — Primary Source Documents

**Historical records showing when, how, and by whose authority the Sabbath was changed to Sunday**

These documents are foundational primary sources — not later Catholic or Protestant commentary, but the actual edicts, canons, and first-hand historical writings from the period when the change occurred:

1. **Emperor Constantine I, Imperial Edict on Sunday Observance** (March 7, A.D. 321):
   - First civil law in history imposing Sunday rest — issued by the pagan Roman Emperor Constantine before any church council formalized the change
   - **Original Text** (*Codex Justinianus*, lib. 3, tit. 12, 3; also found in *Corpus Juris Civilis*):
     > "On the venerable Day of the Sun let the magistrates and people residing in cities rest, and let all workshops be closed. In the country, however, persons engaged in agriculture may freely and lawfully continue their pursuits; because it often happens that another day is not so suitable for grain-sowing or for vine-planting; lest by neglecting the proper moment for such operations the bounty of heaven should be lost."
   - **Key Facts**:
     - The edict uses the pagan term *dies Solis* — "Day of the Sun" — not "the Lord's Day"
     - Constantine was not yet baptised when he issued this decree (he was baptised on his deathbed in 337 AD)
     - The edict exempts farmers — showing it was a civil/economic regulation, not a religious Sabbath transfer
     - This civil Sunday law preceded the Council of Laodicea's church canon by over 40 years
   - **Significance**: The change from Saturday Sabbath to Sunday rest was first imposed by imperial Roman law, under a pagan emperor who worshipped *Sol Invictus* (the Unconquered Sun), before it was codified by any church council — confirming Daniel 7:25's prediction that a power would "think to change times and laws"

   - **Direct Quote** (from *The Seven Ecumenical Councils*, Nicene and Post-Nicene Fathers, Second Series, Vol. 14):

   - **Note on Dating**: *The Convert's Catechism* (item 4 in the Catholic section above) dates this council to A.D. 336; most modern historians date it to A.D. 363–364. Neither date changes the substance: the Church, not Christ or the apostles, made this ruling by ecclesiastical authority

It comes rolling down across the ages with all its original divine authority... You have no business working on the Sabbath unless your ox is in a ditch."
    - **The Irony**: Billy Sunday preached this regarding Sunday — calling it "the Sabbath" — while his own church observed the first day (Sunday), not the seventh day commanded in the Ten Commandments
    - **Significance**: Even the most famous revivalist preacher of the early 20th century taught that the Sabbath commandment is "rolling down across the ages with all its original divine authority" — and unwittingly confirmed that whatever day Christians set aside, it must be kept because God commanded it in a law that does not change

**Significance of Protestant Admissions**:

These Protestant statements reveal a profound inconsistency:

1. **Protestant Doctrine**: *Sola Scriptura* (Bible alone as authority) - The foundational principle of the Protestant Reformation
2. **Protestant Practice**: Sunday observance - Admittedly having no biblical basis

**The Protestant Dilemma**:
- **They reject papal authority** - The Reformation was about returning to biblical authority
- **They keep a papal tradition** - Sunday observance, which they admit has no biblical warrant
- **They admit the inconsistency** - Many Protestant theologians openly acknowledge Sunday has no scriptural basis
- **They continue the practice anyway** - Despite admitting it contradicts *sola scriptura*

**Denominations Represented in This Collection**:
Anglican/Episcopal, Baptist (Southern and General), Brethren, Church of Christ/Disciples of Christ, Congregationalist, Lutheran (including Luther himself and Melanchthon), Methodist (including John Wesley), Moody Bible Institute, Presbyterian/Reformed (including Zwingli, Calvin, Hodge, and Dabney), Quaker, and independent evangelists (Moody, Spurgeon, Billy Sunday). Across more than **15 distinct Protestant traditions** spanning **170+ years** (c. 1824–1997), the answer is the same: no biblical authority for Sunday.

**Key Protestant Admissions**:
- No biblical command for Sunday observance
- Jesus never mentioned changing the Sabbath
- The apostles never authorized the change
- Sunday was "unknown as a day of rest" in the apostolic age (Schaff)
- The change came through church tradition and imperial decree (Constantine, 321 AD)
- The change was formalized by the Council of Laodicea, not by any apostle
- Sunday observance developed through papal/Catholic authority
- Sunday "comes branded with the mark of paganism"
- Protestants inherited Sunday from Rome as "a sacred legacy"
- Even the earliest source describing Sunday worship (Justin Martyr, 155 AD) gives no Scripture for it

**The Catholic Challenge Stands Unanswered**:

Catholic apologists have consistently challenged Protestants: **"If you claim 'Bible only,' why do you keep our Sunday?"**

These Protestant admissions show they have **no answer** to this challenge. They acknowledge:
- Sunday is not commanded in Scripture
- The Sabbath commandment specifies Saturday (seventh day)
- The change came from Catholic Church authority
- By keeping Sunday, they honor a Catholic tradition over biblical commandment

**Prophetic Significance**:

- **Mother**: Catholic Church (openly claims authority to change God's law)
- **Daughters**: Protestant churches (reject Catholic authority but retain Catholic Sunday)
- **"Drunk with her wine"**: Keep the false doctrine (Sunday) while claiming to follow Scripture alone

The Protestant churches' own theologians and leaders admit that by keeping Sunday, they:
1. **Contradict their own doctrine** of *sola scriptura*
2. **Honor Catholic tradition** over biblical commandment
3. **Accept papal authority** in practice while rejecting it in theory
4. **Perpetuate the change** that Daniel 7:25 predicted (the little horn "thinks to change times and law")

When Sunday laws are enforced globally, Protestant churches will have no theological ground to stand on—their own scholars have admitted for over 150 years that Sunday observance has no biblical basis. The Catholic Church can rightly say: **"You've been keeping our day all along. Now it becomes law."**

This is why Revelation 17:5 calls the Protestant churches "daughters" of Babylon—they came out of the mother church during the Reformation, but they **brought the wine (false doctrine) with them**. The most prominent example is Sunday observance, which these Protestant sources frankly admit is tradition, not Scripture.
