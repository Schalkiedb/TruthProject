🇺🇸 United States — Political Push
Washington Correspondent · Conservative Policy Beat · January–March 2026

Heritage Foundation Calls for Blue Laws in "Culture-Wide Manhattan Project"

On January 8, 2026, the Heritage Foundation — the same organisation that authored Project 2025, over 50% of which has already been implemented by the Trump administration — released a companion policy document titled "Saving America by Saving the Family." Framed as a "culture-wide Manhattan Project" to avert demographic collapse before America's 250th anniversary, the report's most controversial proposal calls for the restoration of Blue Laws to establish a "uniform day of rest" on Sundays.

The legal strategy is precise: the Foundation cites the 1961 Supreme Court case McGowan v. Maryland, which upheld Sunday closing laws as constitutional labour regulations, not religious mandates. However, the report's own language may undermine this defence — it explicitly links Sunday rest to combating "spiritual homelessness" and encouraging "religious observance."

"A uniform day of rest… can provide temporal boundaries that help communities set aside time for religious observance, family gatherings, outdoor activities, and rest."

— Heritage Foundation SR323, Page 38, January 8, 2026

The mechanism proposed: amending the Fair Labor Standards Act (FLSA) to require time-and-a-half overtime pay for Sunday workers — creating economic pressure for businesses to close on Sundays without requiring a direct legislative mandate. Saturday-keeping Christians, Jews, Muslims, and other minority faiths would face a double burden: closing on their sacred day and facing economic penalty for working on Sunday.

🇪🇺 European Union

European Sunday Alliance: 100+ Organisations Lobby EU Parliament

Brussels Bureau · EU Elections Watch · March 2024

Founded in 2011 at the European Parliament in Brussels, the European Sunday Alliance (ESA) is a network of over 100 organisations — trade unions, employers' groups, Catholic family associations, environmental groups, and churches — lobbying for Sunday to become a legally protected, work-free day across all 27 EU member states.

On March 1, 2024, ahead of the June 2024 EU Elections, the ESA issued a new manifesto calling on all MEP candidates to commit to Sunday protection. Within days, MEPs from four political groups endorsed it: S&D, EPP, Greens/EFA, and Renew Europe.

⚠ The Religious Liberty Concern — What Critics Are Warning
The Saturday-Keeper Problem

Any law mandating Sunday as a "uniform rest day" places an extra economic burden on the 70M+ Christians who observe Saturday, plus all Jewish (Friday sunset–Saturday sunset), Muslim (Friday), and other faiths. Businesses observing their own Sabbath must close twice — losing two days of revenue against Sunday-observing competitors who close only once. Heritage's document acknowledges this but dismisses it, stating Sunday is the only "realistic" choice for the majority.

The First Amendment Question

The Heritage document's own language may be its legal Achilles heel. By explicitly linking Sunday rest to combating "spiritual homelessness" and encouraging "religious observance," the authors potentially violate the Establishment Clause — the very standard used in McGowan v. Maryland (1961) to uphold Sunday laws only because they were framed as secular labour regulations. A 2026 Sunday law citing religious motivation would face an entirely different constitutional challenge.

The Pattern Warning — 160 Years of Memory

The Seventh-day Adventist Church's institutional memory runs back to the 1880s–1890s, when American Adventists were imprisoned and sentenced to chain-gang labour for tending their farms on Sundays under state Blue Laws. The SDA Church has maintained First Amendment lawyers, published Liberty magazine, and opposed Sunday legislation continuously for 160 years. Their formal statement calls the Heritage proposal "a foot-in-the-door toward influencing states to legislatively enact restrictive worship laws."
