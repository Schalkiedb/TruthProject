# Battle at the End - Part 1: Armageddon Revealed
## A Comprehensive Bible Study Guide

---

## Introduction: The Final Conflict

The Bible reveals a final battle—not fought with weapons of war, but a spiritual conflict over worship and allegiance. This battle has been raging since the beginning, but it reaches its climax at the end of time.

**Armageddon is not what most people think.** It's not a military confrontation in the Middle East. It's not nuclear war. It's not nations fighting over territory.

**Armageddon is the final battle between truth and deception, between God's commandments and Satan's lies, between worship of the Creator and worship of the beast.**

**The choice is simple**: 

**Joshua 24:15**: "And if it seem evil unto you to serve the LORD, **choose you this day whom ye will serve**; whether the gods which your fathers served that were on the other side of the flood, or the gods of the Amorites, in whose land ye dwell: but **as for me and my house, we will serve the LORD**."

**Matthew 4:10**: "...for it is written, **Thou shalt worship the Lord thy God, and Him only shalt thou serve**."

**Foundation Principle**: The battle at the end is a spiritual war over worship—will you serve the Lord God according to His Word, or will you follow the deceptions of Babylon?

**Study Methodology**: Let Scripture reveal the nature of this final conflict, the forces involved, the tactics used, and how God's people will stand victorious through the Word of God.

## Part 1: The Armies Assemble

### Christ and His Army

**Revelation 19:11-16**: "And I saw heaven opened, and behold **a white horse**; and **He that sat upon him was called Faithful and True**, and in righteousness He doth judge and make war. His eyes were as a flame of fire, and on His head were many crowns; and He had a name written, that no man knew, but He Himself. And **He was clothed with a vesture dipped in blood: and His name is called The Word of God**. And the armies which were in heaven followed Him upon white horses, clothed in fine linen, white and clean. And **out of His mouth goeth a sharp sword**, that with it He should smite the nations: and He shall rule them with a rod of iron: and He treadeth the winepress of the fierceness and wrath of Almighty God. And He hath on His vesture and on His thigh a name written, **KING OF KINGS, AND LORD OF LORDS**."

**Christ—The Commander**:
1. **Rides a white horse** (victory, purity, righteousness)
2. **Called "Faithful and True"** (trustworthy, reliable, keeps His promises)
3. **Judges and makes war in righteousness** (just judgment, holy warfare)
4. **Eyes like flames of fire** (sees all, penetrating gaze, nothing hidden)
5. **Many crowns on His head** (supreme authority, King over all kings)
6. **Name no one knows but Himself** (infinite, incomprehensible majesty)
7. **Clothed in a vesture dipped in blood** (the Cross, His sacrifice, redemption)
8. **Name: "The Word of God"** (Scripture personified, God's revealed truth)
9. **Armies follow Him** (redeemed saints, heavenly hosts)
10. **Sharp sword from His mouth** (the Word of God is His weapon)
11. **Rules with rod of iron** (unbreakable authority, cannot be overthrown)
12. **Treads the winepress of God's wrath** (executes final judgment)
13. **KING OF KINGS, AND LORD OF LORDS** (supreme sovereignty)

**His Weapon**: The Word of God (the sword from His mouth)

### The Beast and His Army

**Revelation 19:19**: "And I saw **the beast, and the kings of the earth, and their armies, gathered together to make war against Him that sat on the horse, and against His army**."

**The Opposition**:
1. **The beast** (papal Rome—the antichrist system)
2. **The kings of the earth** (political powers allied with the beast)
3. **Their armies** (deceived masses who follow the beast)

**They make war against**:
- Christ (Him that sat on the horse)
- His army (God's faithful people)

**This is the battle of Armageddon**—the final showdown between Christ and the antichrist system.

## Part 2: How the Armies Are Gathered

### Three Unclean Spirits

**Revelation 16:13-14**: "And I saw **three unclean spirits like frogs** come out of the mouth of the dragon, and out of the mouth of the beast, and out of the mouth of the false prophet. For **they are the spirits of devils, working miracles**, which go forth unto the kings of the earth and of the whole world, **to gather them to the battle of that great day of God Almighty**."

**The Gathering Mechanism**:
1. **Three unclean spirits** (demons, fallen angels, evil spirits)
2. **Like frogs** (unclean animals, croaking deception, camouflage)
3. **From three sources**:
   - **Dragon** (Satan, paganism, spiritualism)
   - **Beast** (papal Rome, apostate Christianity)
   - **False prophet** (apostate Protestantism—USA, the image of the beast)

**Their Method**:
- **Working miracles** (supernatural signs and wonders)
- **Deception** (counterfeit power to validate false claims)
- **Global reach** ("kings of the earth and of the whole world")

**Revelation 13:14**: "And **deceiveth them that dwell on the earth by...those miracles**...saying to them that dwell on the earth, that they should make an image to the beast."

**The whole world will be deceived by miraculous signs**—and these signs will lead them to worship the beast and his image.

### The Gathering Place

**Revelation 16:16**: "And he gathered them together into a place called in the Hebrew tongue **Armageddon**."

**Armageddon** = "Mount of Megiddo" or "Mountain of Assembly"

**Not a literal place**—symbolic of the final gathering for the battle over worship and God's law.

**Old Testament Megiddo** was the site of many decisive battles in Israel's history—now used symbolically for the ultimate battle between good and evil.

**The weapon in this battle**:

**The battle is fought with truth vs. lies, Scripture vs. tradition, God's Word vs. human authority.**

## Part 6: Satan's Tactics—Deception

### The Great Deceiver

**Revelation 12:9**: "And the great dragon was cast out, that old serpent, called the Devil, and Satan, **which deceiveth the whole world**: he was cast out into the earth, and his angels were cast out with him."

**Satan's primary weapon** = **Deception**

**He deceives "the whole world"**—except God's remnant who keep His commandments and have the testimony of Jesus.

### Masquerading as Light

**2 Corinthians 11:13-15**: "For such are **false apostles, deceitful workers, transforming themselves into apostles of Christ**. And no wonder! For **Satan himself transforms himself into an angel of light**. Therefore it is no great thing if **his ministers also transform themselves into ministers of righteousness**, whose end will be according to their works."

**Satan's Method**:
1. **Transforms himself into an angel of light** (appears good, holy, righteous)
2. **False apostles** (claim to represent Christ but teach lies)
3. **Deceitful workers** (appear to be working for God but serve Satan)
4. **Ministers of righteousness** (seem righteous, preach morality, but deny God's truth)

**Satan doesn't appear as a red devil with horns and pitchfork**—he comes as an "angel of light," speaking religious language, performing miracles, appearing righteous.

**This is why the deception is so powerful**—it looks and sounds Christian.

### False Christs and False Prophets

**Matthew 24:24**: "For **false christs and false prophets will rise and show great signs and wonders to deceive, if possible, even the elect**."

**End-time deception**:
- **False christs** (claiming to be Christ or claiming to represent Him)
- **False prophets** (claiming to speak for God)
- **Great signs and wonders** (miracles that appear supernatural)
- **Purpose**: To deceive **even the elect** (God's chosen people)

**Only those who know God's Word will not be deceived**—the signs and wonders will be so convincing that feeling, emotion, and appearances cannot be trusted.

**Only Scripture can protect you.**

### Sincere but Deceived

**John 16:2**: "They will put you out of the synagogues; yes, **the time is coming that whoever kills you will think that he offers God service**."

**The persecutors will believe they are serving God**:
- Sincere in their conviction
- Genuinely believe they are doing right
- Think they are defending God's honor
- Will persecute God's people while claiming to be righteous

**This is the danger of tradition and deception**—people can be sincerely wrong, genuinely convinced they are serving God while actually opposing Him.

## Part 10: Tower of Babel—The First Babylon

### Global Unity Against God

**Genesis 11:1-4**: "Now the whole earth had one language and one speech. And it came to pass, as they journeyed from the east, that they found a plain in the land of Shinar, and they dwelt there. Then they said to one another, 'Come, let us make bricks and bake them thoroughly.' They had brick for stone, and they had asphalt for mortar. And they said, '**Come, let us build ourselves a city, and a tower whose top is in the heavens; let us make a name for ourselves**, lest we be scattered abroad over the face of the whole earth.'"

**Characteristics of Babel**:
1. **One language** (universal communication)
2. **One speech** (unified message)
3. **Human effort** ("let us build")
4. **Reaching to heaven** (attempting to reach God by works)
5. **Making a name for themselves** (self-exaltation, pride)
6. **Resisting God's command** (God said "fill the earth"; they said "let's not be scattered")

**Babel = man's attempt to reach heaven by his own works, refusing to obey God's command.**

### God's Response

**Genesis 11:5-9**: "But the Lord came down to see the city and the tower which the sons of men had built. And the Lord said, 'Indeed the people are one and they all have one language, and this is what they begin to do; now nothing that they propose to do will be withheld from them. Come, let Us go down and there **confuse their language**, that they may not understand one another's speech.' So the Lord **scattered them abroad** from there over the face of all the earth, and they ceased building the city. Therefore its name is called **Babel**, because there the Lord confused the language of all the earth; and from there the Lord scattered them abroad over the face of all the earth."

**God's intervention**:
1. **Confused their language** (broke their unity)
2. **Scattered them** (fulfilled His original command)
3. **Named it Babel** (confusion)

**Babel became Babylon**—the symbol of human rebellion against God, false worship, and confusion.
