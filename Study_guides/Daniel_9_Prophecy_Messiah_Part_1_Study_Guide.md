## Introduction: A Prophecy So Accurate It Had to Be Hidden

Daniel 9:24-27 contains **the most mathematically precise prophecy in the entire Bible**. It predicts:

1. **The exact year** the Messiah would begin His ministry
2. **The exact year** the Messiah would be crucified
3. **The exact duration** of His ministry
4. **The end of the Jewish nation's special probation**

**This prophecy is so accurate that Jewish rabbis forbid reading it.**

**Why?** Because when you calculate the dates, there is only **one person in history** who fulfills this prophecy: **Jesus Christ of Nazareth**.

**Historical note**: After the destruction of Jerusalem in 70 CE, Jewish tradition strongly discouraged attempts to calculate the dates in Daniel 9:24-27. Jewish scholar Rabbi Samuel b. Naḥman (3rd century CE), quoted in the Talmud (Sanhedrin 97b), said: *"May the bones of those who calculate the end times be blasted away!"* — a reflection of how seriously the rabbis guarded against date-setting based on Daniel. Many scholars believe this reflects an awareness that the prophecy points directly to Jesus as the Messiah.

**In this study, we will**:
- Examine the prophecy verse by verse
- Calculate the dates using biblical principles
- Prove Jesus fulfilled this prophecy to the exact year
- Show why this is irrefutable evidence Jesus is the Messiah

**Key Principle**: We will use **only the Bible** to interpret this prophecy. Scripture will interpret Scripture—no human speculation, just God's Word.

## Part 4: Understanding Prophetic Time

Before calculating the dates, we must understand **how time works in biblical prophecy**.

### The Day-Year Principle

**Key biblical principle**: In symbolic prophecy, **1 prophetic day = 1 literal year**

**Biblical proof**:

> **Numbers 14:34** - "After the number of the days in which ye searched the land, even forty days, **each day for a year**, shall you bear your iniquities, even forty years, and ye shall know my breach of promise."

**Clear statement**: "Each day for a year"

> **Ezekiel 4:6** - "And when thou hast accomplished them, lie again on thy right side, and thou shalt bear the iniquity of the house of Judah forty days: **I have appointed thee each day for a year**."

**Repeated principle**: "Each day for a year"

**Application to Daniel 9:24**:
- 70 weeks = 70 × 7 days = **490 days**
- Using the day-year principle: 490 days = **490 years**

**Therefore**: **70 weeks = 490 years**

---

### The Biblical Year: 360 Days

**How many days in a biblical year?**

Modern calendar = 365.25 days  
**Biblical calendar** = **360 days** (12 months × 30 days each)

**Biblical proof**:

> **Genesis 7:11** - "In the six hundredth year of Noah's life, in the second month, the seventeenth day of the month, the same day were all the fountains of the great deep broken up, and the windows of heaven were opened."

> **Genesis 8:3-4** - "And the waters returned from off the earth continually: and after the end of the **hundred and fifty days** the waters were abated. And the ark rested in the **seventh month, on the seventeenth day of the month**, upon the mountains of Ararat."

**Timeline**:
- Flood began: **Second month, 17th day**
- Waters abated: **Seventh month, 17th day**
- Duration: **150 days**

**Calculation**:
- From 2nd month (day 17) to 7th month (day 17) = **5 months**
- 150 days ÷ 5 months = **30 days per month**

**Conclusion**: Biblical months = **30 days**, and 12 months × 30 days = **360 days per year**

This is further confirmed in Revelation, where the same 3.5-year period is described as both **"forty-two months"** (Revelation 11:2; 13:5) and **"a thousand two hundred and threescore days"** (Revelation 11:3; 12:6) — 1,260 ÷ 42 = exactly **30 days per month**.

**Confirmed**: Biblical prophetic year = **360 days**

---

### The Math: 70 Weeks = 490 Years

**Daniel 9:24**: "Seventy weeks are determined..."

**Calculation**:
- 70 weeks × 7 days = **490 days**
- Day-year principle: **490 years**

**Daniel 9:25**: "...from the going forth of the commandment to restore and to build Jerusalem unto the Messiah the Prince shall be seven weeks, and threescore and two weeks"

**Calculation**:
- 7 weeks + 62 weeks = **69 weeks**
- 69 weeks × 7 days = **483 days**
- Day-year principle: **483 years**

**Summary**:
- **Total prophecy**: 70 weeks = **490 years**
- **To the Messiah**: 69 weeks = **483 years**
- **Remaining**: 1 week = **7 years**

## Part 5: The Starting Point - 457 BCE

### Finding the Decree to Restore Jerusalem

**The prophecy begins**: "From the going forth of the commandment to restore and to build Jerusalem" (Daniel 9:25)

**Question**: Which decree? There were several:

1. **Cyrus's decree** (538 BCE) - Ezra 1:1-4 (allowed Jews to return, rebuild temple)
2. **Darius's decree** (520 BCE) - Ezra 6:1-12 (reaffirmed Cyrus's decree)
3. **Artaxerxes's decree** (457 BCE) - Ezra 7:11-26 (**restored Jerusalem's full government and walls**)

**The correct decree**: **Artaxerxes's decree in 457 BCE**

**Why?** Only Artaxerxes's decree authorized:
- Full restoration of Jerusalem's government
- Rebuilding the city walls and streets
- Reestablishing Jewish law and judiciary
- Civil authority (not just temple worship)

**Biblical confirmation**:

> **Ezra 6:14** - "And the elders of the Jews builded, and they prospered through the prophesying of Haggai the prophet and Zechariah the son of Iddo. And they builded, and finished it, according to the commandment of the God of Israel, and according to the **commandment of Cyrus, and Darius, and Artaxerxes king of Persia**."

**All three decrees** (Cyrus, Darius, Artaxerxes) worked together to restore Jerusalem, but **Artaxerxes's decree was the final, complete authorization**.

**Date confirmed**: **457 BCE** (7th year of Artaxerxes's reign - Ezra 7:7-8)

## Part 6: Calculating to the Messiah - 27 CE

### The Math: 457 BCE + 483 Years = ?

**Starting point**: 457 BCE  
**Duration to Messiah**: 483 years (69 weeks)  
**Ending point**: ?

**Calculation**:
- 457 BCE + 483 years = **26 CE**

**Wait—why not 26 CE?** Because there is **no year zero** in the BC/AD calendar system. It goes from 1 BCE directly to 1 CE.

**Correct calculation**:
- 457 BCE + 483 years = 27 CE (accounting for no year zero)

**Result**: The Messiah should appear/be anointed in **27 CE**.

## Part 8: The Crucifixion Prophecy - Genesis 3:15

### The First Messianic Prophecy

> **Genesis 3:15** - "And I will put enmity between you [serpent] and the woman, and between your seed and her Seed; **He shall bruise your head, and you shall bruise His heel**."

**Context**: Immediately after Adam and Eve sinned, God prophesied the plan of salvation.

**Key elements**:
1. **"Her Seed"** = The Messiah (born of a woman—virgin birth)
2. **"He shall bruise your head"** = Messiah will deliver a fatal blow to Satan (crush his power through the cross)
3. **"You shall bruise His heel"** = Satan will wound the Messiah (crucifixion), but it's not fatal (resurrection)

**Head wound vs. Heel wound**:
- **Head wound** = Fatal, permanent (Satan's defeat at the cross)
- **Heel wound** = Painful but temporary (Jesus' death, but He rose again)

---

> **Daniel 2:40** - "And the fourth kingdom shall be strong as iron: forasmuch as **iron breaketh in pieces and subdueth all things**: and as iron that breaketh all these, shall it **break in pieces and bruise**."

**The fourth kingdom** (Rome) = characterized by **iron**

**"Break in pieces and bruise"** = Rome's brutal military tactics

**Connection to Genesis 3:15**:
- **Rome** (iron kingdom) = instrument of Satan
- **Rome crucified Jesus** = "bruised His heel"
