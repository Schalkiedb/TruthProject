# The Mark of the Beast: The Final Test of Loyalty
## A Comprehensive Bible Study Guide

## Introduction: The Most Solemn Warning in Scripture

The mark of the beast carries the most severe warning in all of Scripture. It's not a mystery to be feared but a truth to be understood. God has clearly revealed what the mark is, who enforces it, and how to avoid it. This isn't speculation—it's biblical prophecy backed by historical evidence and the admissions of the very power that claims authority to enforce it.

**This study will connect everything we've learned**: the little horn (papal Rome), the second beast (America), the Three Angels' Messages, Creation, the Sabbath, and God's law. All of these converge at one point—**the mark of the beast**.

**Foundation Principle**: The mark of the beast is a mark of authority. It represents choosing human tradition over God's commandments, specifically regarding worship.

**Study Methodology**: Let Scripture define the mark, examine the seal of God as its counterpart, review the historical evidence, and understand the choice every person must make.

## Part 1: Keep God's Commandments

### The Call to Treasure God's Law

**Proverbs 7:1-3**: "My son, **keep My words, and treasure My commands within you**. Keep My commands and live, and My law as the apple of your eye. **Bind them on your fingers; write them on the tablet of your heart**."

**Three Actions Required**:
1. **Keep** God's words (guard, observe, obey)
2. **Treasure** His commands (value highly, cherish)
3. **Write them on your heart** (internalize, make them part of you)

**"Apple of your eye"** = something precious, carefully protected, constantly watched over

**"Bind them on your fingers"** = actions reflect God's law
**"Write them on the tablet of your heart"** = mind/beliefs shaped by God's law

**This is preparation** for what's coming—when the mark is enforced, those who have God's law written on their hearts will not compromise.

### The Mark in Hand and Forehead

**Revelation 13:16-17**: "And he causeth all, both small and great, rich and poor, free and bond, to receive **a mark in their right hand, or in their foreheads**: And that no man might buy or sell, save he that had **the mark, or the name of the beast, or the number of his name**."

**Contrast**:
- **God's law**: Written on heart and hand (Proverbs 7:3, Deuteronomy 6:8)
- **Beast's mark**: Received in forehead or hand (Revelation 13:16)

**The battle is over whose authority you acknowledge**:
- **Forehead** = what you believe, your mind's allegiance
- **Hand** = what you do, your actions' conformity

**Both locations indicate worship and obedience**—but to whom?

## Part 5: The Fourth Man in the Fire

### Divine Deliverance

**Daniel 3:23-25**: "And these three men, Shadrach, Meshach, and Abednego, fell down bound into the midst of the burning fiery furnace. Then Nebuchadnezzar the king was astonied, and rose up in haste, and spake, and said unto his counsellors, Did not we cast three men bound into the midst of the fire? They answered and said unto the king, True, O king. He answered and said, Lo, **I see four men loose, walking in the midst of the fire, and they have no hurt; and the form of the fourth is like the Son of God**."

**The Fourth Man** — Nebuchadnezzar described the form as "like the Son of God" (KJV). The Aramaic *bar-elah* literally means "son of the gods" — the language of a pagan king describing a divine being. Most Bible scholars identify this figure as the **pre-incarnate Christ** (a Christophany), consistent with Christ's appearances throughout the Old Testament (Genesis 18; Joshua 5:13-15; Isaiah 6). Whoever He was, His presence in the fire is the point: God did not abandon His faithful servants.

**What happened**:
- Bonds burned off (set free in the fire)
- No hurt (protected in the trial)
- Walking, not writhing (peace in persecution)
- Jesus with them (not alone)

**Application**: When God's people face the mark of the beast:
- Jesus will be with them
- They'll be free even in bondage
- No ultimate harm will come
- They'll have peace in the trial

## Part 11: What Is the Seal?

### Sealed by the Holy Spirit

**Ephesians 4:30**: "And **grieve not the Holy Spirit of God, whereby ye are sealed unto the day of redemption**."

**Sealed by** = the Holy Spirit
**Sealed unto** = day of redemption (Christ's return)

**How not to grieve the Spirit?** Obey God's Word, keep His commandments, walk in truth.

### The Law Sealed

**Isaiah 8:16**: "Bind up the testimony, **seal the law among my disciples**."

**Command**: Seal the law (make it secure, protect it, authenticate it)

**Where?** Among God's disciples (those who follow Him)

**The seal is connected to God's law**—specifically, it authenticates the law, showing its authority.

### What Makes a Seal Official?

A governmental seal contains three elements:
1. **Name** of the authority
2. **Title/Office** of the authority
3. **Territory/Domain** of the authority

**Example - U.S. Presidential Seal**:
1. Name: [President's name]
2. Title: President
3. Territory: United States of America

**Where is God's seal in His law?**

God's name (LORD/YHWH) appears throughout the commandments, and His authority is implied throughout. But only one commandment combines all three seal elements — *name*, *title*, and *territory* — explicitly in a single statement:

**Exodus 20:8-11** (Fourth Commandment—the Sabbath):
- **Verse 8**: "Remember the Sabbath day, to keep it holy."
- **Verse 10**: "but the seventh day is the Sabbath of **the LORD thy God**..." [NAME]
- **Verse 11**: "For in six days **the LORD made** heaven and earth..." [TITLE: Creator/Maker] [TERRITORY: heaven, earth, sea]

**The Sabbath commandment is God's seal**:
1. **Name**: LORD (YHWH)
2. **Title**: Creator ("made")
3. **Territory**: heaven, earth, sea, all that is in them

**No other commandment contains all three elements.**

**The Sabbath** = the seal of God's law, authenticating His authority as Creator.

## Part 13: Jesus and the Law

### Jesus Didn't Abolish the Law

**Matthew 5:17-18, 20**: "Do not think that I came to **destroy the Law** or the Prophets. **I did not come to destroy but to fulfill**. For assuredly, I say to you, **till heaven and earth pass away, one jot or one tittle will by no means pass from the law** till all is fulfilled... For I say to you, that unless your righteousness exceeds the righteousness of the scribes and Pharisees, you will by no means enter the kingdom of heaven."

**Jesus' clear statement**:
- **Did NOT come to destroy the law** (it remains binding)
- Came to **fulfill** (live out perfectly, demonstrate properly)
- Law remains **until heaven and earth pass** (still here? law still binding)
- **Not one jot or tittle** (smallest letter or stroke) removed

**Jot** = yod (י), smallest Hebrew letter
**Tittle** = tiny stroke distinguishing similar letters

**Meaning**: Not even the smallest detail of God's law is removed.

**Revelation 22:14-15**: "**Blessed are they that do His commandments**, that they may have right to the tree of life, and may enter in through the gates into the city. For without are dogs, and sorcerers, and whoremongers, and murderers, and idolaters, and whosoever loveth and maketh a lie."

**Entrance requirement**: Doing God's commandments (all ten, by His grace)

**Those excluded**: Those who make/love a lie (claim to follow God while breaking His law)

### Jesus Magnified the Law

**Matthew 5:21-22**: "You have heard that it was said to those of old, 'You shall not murder, and whoever murders will be in danger of the judgment.' **But I say to you that whoever is angry with his brother without a cause shall be in danger of the judgment**..."

**Matthew 5:27-28**: "You have heard that it was said to those of old, 'You shall not commit adultery.' **But I say to you that whoever looks at a woman to lust for her has already committed adultery with her in his heart**."

**Jesus' teaching**: The law goes **deeper than external actions**—it governs **heart attitudes**.

- Murder includes anger
- Adultery includes lust
- External obedience isn't enough—heart must be transformed

**This is why we need Christ's righteousness**—we can't keep the law perfectly in our own strength. But by His grace, His Spirit writes the law on our hearts.

## Part 16: The Little Horn Changes Times and Laws

### Daniel's Prophecy

**Daniel 7:25**: "And he shall speak great words against the Most High, and shall wear out the saints of the Most High, and **think to change times and laws**: and they shall be given into his hand until a time and times and the dividing of time."

**The little horn (papal Rome) will**:

- Specifically: **times and laws** (when and what of God's commandments)

**Question**: What "time" is in God's law?

**Answer**: The Sabbath—seventh day, specific time set apart by God.

**Which law has the little horn changed?** The Fourth Commandment (Sabbath from seventh day to first day).

### Constantine's Sunday Law (321 CE)

**321 CE**: Emperor Constantine declared:

> "On the venerable day of the Sun let the magistrates and people residing in cities rest, and let all workshops be closed."

**"Venerable Day of the Sun"** = Sunday (day dedicated to sun worship)

**Historical Context**: Constantine was a devotee of **Sol Invictus** ("the Unconquered Sun"), the official sun cult of the late Roman Empire. His Sunday edict used the phrase "venerable day of the Sun" (*dies Solis*), directly referencing this solar religion. The merging of solar worship with emerging Christendom gave Sunday its religious prestige in Roman imperial culture.

**Note**: This was a civil law first, later adopted and enforced by the church.
