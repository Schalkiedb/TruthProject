In Part 1, we saw the armies gathering for the final battle—Christ with the sword of His Word, and the beast with his lying wonders and deceptions. We saw how Babylon intoxicates the world with her false doctrines, preparing them to receive the mark of the beast.

**Now in Part 2, we see**:
- The conspiracy against God's Sabbath
- Belshazzar's feast—ancient Babylon's fall
- Revelation 18—end-time Babylon's fall
- The parallel with Tyre—pride, riches, and destruction
- The prince of Tyre and the king of Tyre—human and satanic
- God's final call: "Come out of her, My people"
- The gospel of grace and obedience
- The seal of God and the patience of the saints

## Part 1: The Roaring Lion—A Conspiracy Against the Sabbath

### The Devil as a Roaring Lion

**1 Peter 5:8**: "Be sober, be vigilant; because **your adversary the devil walks about like a roaring lion, seeking whom he may devour**."

**Satan's tactics**:
- **Walks about** (actively searching for victims)
- **Like a roaring lion** (fierce, intimidating, terrifying)
- **Seeking whom he may devour** (looking for those he can destroy)

**We must be**:
- **Sober** (clear-minded, not drunk on Babylon's wine)
- **Vigilant** (watchful, alert, on guard)

### The Conspiracy of Prophets and Priests

**Ezekiel 22:25-26**: "There is **a conspiracy of her prophets** in the midst thereof, **like a roaring lion ravening the prey**; they have **devoured souls**; they have taken the treasure and precious things; they have made her many widows in the midst thereof. Her priests have **violated My law**, and have **profaned Mine holy things**: they have **put no difference between the holy and profane**, neither have they showed difference between the unclean and the clean, and have **hid their eyes from My Sabbaths**, and **I am profaned among them**."

**The Conspiracy**:
1. **Prophets** (religious leaders, teachers)
2. **Like a roaring lion** (fierce, destructive—same description as Satan)
3. **Devoured souls** (destroyed people spiritually)
4. **Taken treasure and precious things** (stolen what belongs to God)

**The Priests**:
1. **Violated God's law** (broken the Ten Commandments)
2. **Profaned holy things** (treated sacred things as common)
3. **No difference between holy and profane** (mixed truth with error)
4. **No difference between clean and unclean** (compromised standards)
5. **Hid their eyes from the Sabbaths** (ignored, concealed, or changed the Sabbath)
6. **Result**: "**I am profaned among them**" (God is dishonored)

**This is the heart of the conspiracy—hiding the Sabbath truth, violating God's law, profaning holy things.**

**Religious leaders have conspired to hide the Sabbath and replace it with Sunday**—profaning God's holy day and causing the world to break the fourth commandment.

## Part 2: Belshazzar's Feast—Ancient Babylon's Fall

### The Great Feast

**Daniel 5:1**: "**Belshazzar the king made a great feast** to a thousand of his lords, and drank wine before the thousand."

**Belshazzar** = last king of Babylon before its fall (539 BC)

**The feast**:
- 1,000 lords (massive celebration)
- Drinking wine (intoxication, impaired judgment)
- False sense of security (Babylon thought it was invincible)

### Desecrating the Holy Vessels

**Daniel 5:3**: "Then they brought **the golden vessels that were taken out of the temple of the house of God which was at Jerusalem**; and the king, and his princes, his wives, and his concubines, **drank in them**."

**The sin**:
1. **Took vessels from God's temple** (stolen during Jerusalem's destruction)
2. **Drank wine in them** (used holy things for profane purposes)
3. **The king, princes, wives, concubines** (everyone participated in the blasphemy)

**Parallel to Ezekiel 22:25-26**:
- "Taken the treasure and precious things" (the temple vessels)
- "No difference between the holy and profane" (using holy vessels for drunken revelry)
- "Hid their eyes from My Sabbaths" (ignored God's holy things)

### Music and Wine—Ignoring God's Work

**Isaiah 5:12**: "**The harp and the strings, the tambourine and flute, and wine are in their feasts**; but **they do not regard the work of the Lord, nor consider the operation of His hands**."

**At Belshazzar's feast**:
- Music and wine (emotional manipulation, distraction)
- Ignoring God's work (no thought of God's judgment)
- Celebrating while destruction is near

**Daniel 5:4**: "They drank wine, and **praised the gods of gold, and of silver, of brass, of iron, of wood, and of stone**."

**They worshiped false gods**:
- Material wealth (gold, silver, brass, iron)
- Dead idols (wood and stone)
- Not the living God who holds their breath

### The Handwriting on the Wall

**Daniel 5:5-6**: "In the same hour came forth **fingers of a man's hand, and wrote** over against the candlestick upon the plaister of the wall of the king's palace: and the king saw the part of the hand that wrote. Then **the king's countenance was changed, and his thoughts troubled him**, so that the joints of his loins were loosed, and his knees smote one against another."

**God's intervention**:
- Mysterious hand appeared
- Writing on the wall (visible judgment)
- King terrified (fear, trembling, loss of control)

### The Interpretation—Babylon Weighed and Found Wanting

**Daniel 5:26-27**: "This is the interpretation of the thing: **MENE; God hath numbered thy kingdom, and finished it**. **TEKEL; Thou art weighed in the balances, and art found wanting**."

**God's verdict**:
1. **MENE** (numbered)—God has counted Babylon's days, and they're over
2. **TEKEL** (weighed)—God has judged Babylon, and it doesn't measure up
3. **PERES** (divided—v. 28)—Babylon's kingdom will be divided and given to others

**That very night, Babylon fell to the Medes and Persians** (Daniel 5:30-31).

**Ancient Babylon fell suddenly, in one night, while feasting and feeling secure.**

**End-time Babylon will fall the same way—suddenly, unexpectedly, while the world feels secure.**

## Part 3: Revelation 18—End-Time Babylon's Fall

### Another Angel with Great Power

**Revelation 18:1**: "And after these things I saw **another angel come down from heaven, having great power**; and **the earth was lightened with his glory**."

**This is the "loud cry"**—the final, powerful proclamation of the Three Angels' Messages (Revelation 14:6-12) with increased intensity.

**Characteristics**:
- **Great power** (Holy Spirit's latter rain)
- **Earth lightened with glory** (global, unmistakable, compelling)

### Babylon Is Fallen

**Revelation 18:2**: "And he cried mightily with a strong voice, saying, **Babylon the great is fallen, is fallen**, and is become **the habitation of devils**, and the hold of **every foul spirit**, and a cage of **every unclean and hateful bird**."

**"Babylon is fallen"** (repeated twice for emphasis—complete, certain fall)

**Babylon has become**:
1. **Habitation of devils** (controlled by demons)
2. **Hold of every foul spirit** (filled with evil spirits)
3. **Cage of every unclean bird** (symbolic of unclean doctrines and practices)

**Moral fall** (already fallen into apostasy) → **Physical fall** (coming destruction)

### All Nations Drunk with Her Wine

**Revelation 18:3**: "For **all nations have drunk of the wine of the wrath of her fornication**, and the kings of the earth have committed fornication with her, and the merchants of the earth are waxed rich through the abundance of her delicacies."

**Three groups involved**:
1. **All nations** (deceived by her false doctrines—the "wine")
2. **Kings of the earth** (political powers united with religious power—"fornication")
3. **Merchants of the earth** (economic powers enriched by the system)

**This is global**—religious, political, and economic powers all united in Babylon's system.

## Part 4: "Come Out of Her, My People"

### God's Final Call

**Revelation 18:4**: "And I heard another voice from heaven, saying, **Come out of her, My people**, that ye be not partakers of her sins, and that ye receive not of her plagues."

**God's command**:
1. **"Come out of her"** (separate from Babylon's system)
2. **"My people"** (God still has people in Babylon—sincere believers who don't yet know the truth)
3. **Purpose 1**: "That ye be not partakers of her sins" (don't participate in her false worship)
4. **Purpose 2**: "That ye receive not of her plagues" (escape the coming judgment)

**This is God's mercy**—He warns His people to escape before it's too late.

**Coming out of Babylon means**:
- Rejecting her false doctrines (wine)
- Refusing her false worship (Sunday observance)
- Separating from church-state union
- Following God's Word alone (Sola Scriptura)
- Keeping God's commandments (including the Sabbath)

### Her Sins Have Reached Heaven

**Revelation 18:5**: "For **her sins have reached unto heaven**, and **God hath remembered her iniquities**."

**Like the Tower of Babel** (Genesis 11:4)—building to heaven in rebellion against God.

**Like Sodom and Gomorrah** (Genesis 18:20-21)—the cry reached heaven, and God came down to judge.

**God has kept a record of every sin**—martyrs' blood, souls deceived, truth suppressed, Sabbath violated—and now the time of judgment has come.

### Double Judgment

**Revelation 18:6**: "**Reward her even as she rewarded you, and double unto her double** according to her works: in the cup which she hath filled fill to her double."

**Babylon persecuted God's people**—now she receives double judgment.

**The cup she filled with the blood of martyrs**—now filled double with God's wrath.

### Pride Before the Fall

**Revelation 18:7**: "How much she hath glorified herself, and lived deliciously, so much torment and sorrow give her: for **she saith in her heart, I sit a queen, and am no widow, and shall see no sorrow**."

**Babylon's pride**:
1. **Glorified herself** (self-exaltation)
2. **Lived deliciously** (luxury, indulgence, wealth)
3. **"I sit a queen"** (claims sovereignty)
4. **"Am no widow"** (feels secure, thinks she can't lose)
5. **"Shall see no sorrow"** (thinks judgment won't come)

**This is the same pride that led to Babel, Sodom, ancient Babylon, and every fallen system.**

**Proverbs 16:18**: "Pride goeth before destruction, and an haughty spirit before a fall."

### Sudden Destruction in One Day

**Revelation 18:8**: "Therefore shall **her plagues come in one day**, death, and mourning, and famine; and **she shall be utterly burned with fire**: for strong is the Lord God who judgeth her."

**"In one day"**—sudden, swift judgment (like ancient Babylon falling in one night)

**The plagues**:
- Death
- Mourning
- Famine
- Utterly burned with fire (complete destruction)

**Why?** "For **strong is the Lord God who judgeth her**"—God's justice cannot be escaped.

## Part 5: The World Mourns Babylon's Fall

### Kings Lament

**Revelation 18:9-10**: "And **the kings of the earth**, who have committed fornication and lived deliciously with her, shall bewail her, and lament for her, when they shall see the smoke of her burning, **Standing afar off for the fear of her torment**, saying, Alas, alas, **that great city Babylon, that mighty city! for in one hour is thy judgment come**."

**The kings**:
- Committed fornication (church-state union)
- Lived deliciously (enjoyed wealth and power through the system)
- Bewail and lament (grieve, mourn)
- **Stand afar off** (afraid to get too close—don't want the same judgment)
- **"In one hour"** (swift, sudden judgment)

### Merchants Mourn

**Revelation 18:11-16**: "And **the merchants of the earth** shall weep and mourn over her; for **no man buyeth their merchandise any more**: The merchandise of gold, and silver, and precious stones, and of pearls, and fine linen, and purple, and silk, and scarlet, and all thyine wood, and all manner vessels of ivory, and all manner vessels of most precious wood, and of brass, and iron, and marble, And cinnamon, and odours, and ointments, and frankincense, and wine, and oil, and fine flour, and wheat, and beasts, and sheep, and horses, and chariots, **and slaves, and souls of men**... The merchants of these things, which were made rich by her, shall stand afar off for the fear of her torment, weeping and wailing, And saying, Alas, alas, that great city, that was clothed in fine linen, and purple, and scarlet, and decked with gold, and precious stones, and pearls!"

**The merchants**:
- Made rich by Babylon's system
- Weep because **no one buys their merchandise** (economic collapse)
- List includes material wealth (gold, silver, precious stones)
- List includes luxury items (purple, scarlet, silk, spices)
- **List ends with "slaves, and souls of men"** (trafficking in human souls—the ultimate merchandise)

**Babylon trades in souls**—deceiving people, controlling them, leading them to eternal destruction for profit and power.

### Shipmasters and Sailors Mourn

**Revelation 18:17-19**: "For **in one hour so great riches is come to nought**. And every **shipmaster**, and all the company in ships, and **sailors**, and as many as trade by sea, stood afar off, And cried when they saw the smoke of her burning, saying, **What city is like unto this great city**! And **they cast dust on their heads**, and cried, weeping and wailing, saying, Alas, alas, that great city, wherein were made rich all that had ships in the sea by reason of her costliness! for **in one hour is she made desolate**."

**Shipmasters and sailors** (those who transported goods, facilitated trade):
- Stood afar off (fear of judgment)
- Cried when they saw the smoke (witnessed destruction)
- "What city is like unto this great city!" (astonishment at the fall)
- Cast dust on their heads (sign of deep mourning)
- Weeping and wailing (intense grief)
- **"In one hour is she made desolate"** (swift judgment)

### Complete and Permanent Destruction

**Revelation 18:21**: "And a mighty angel took up a stone like a great millstone, and cast it into the sea, saying, **Thus with violence shall that great city Babylon be thrown down, and shall be found no more at all**."

**Like a millstone thrown into the sea**:
- Sudden
- Violent
- Irreversible
- Permanent

**"Shall be found no more at all"**—Babylon's destruction is eternal.

### Blood of Prophets and Saints

**Revelation 18:24**: "And **in her was found the blood of prophets, and of saints, and of all that were slain upon the earth**."

**Babylon is guilty of**:
- Killing prophets (God's messengers)
- Killing saints (God's people)
- All that were slain (all martyrs throughout history)

**The Inquisition, religious wars, persecution of Sabbath-keepers, martyrs burned at the stake—all trace back to Babylon's system.**

**God will avenge His people's blood.**

## Part 6: The Parallel with Tyre—Pride, Riches, and Destruction

### Seventy Years and Fornication

**Jeremiah 29:10**: "For thus saith the LORD, That **after seventy years** be accomplished at Babylon I will visit you, and perform my good word toward you, in causing you to return to this place."

**Isaiah 23:17**: "And it shall come to pass **after the end of seventy years**, that the LORD will visit **Tyre**, and she shall turn to her hire, and shall **commit fornication with all the kingdoms of the world** upon the face of the earth."

**Parallel**:
- Both Babylon and Tyre judged for 70 years
- Tyre commits fornication with all kingdoms (like Babylon in Revelation 17-18)

**Tyre** = ancient merchant city, symbol of pride and commercial power

**Ezekiel 27-28** prophesies against Tyre, using language strikingly similar to Revelation 18's description of Babylon's fall.

### Tyre's Fall—Merchants and Mariners Mourn

**Ezekiel 27:27-33**: "Thy riches, and thy fairs, thy merchandise, thy mariners, and thy pilots...shall fall into the midst of the seas in the day of thy ruin. The suburbs shall shake at the sound of the cry of thy pilots. And all that handle the oar, the mariners, and all the pilots of the sea, shall come down from their ships, they shall stand upon the land; And shall cause their voice to be heard against thee, and shall cry bitterly, and **shall cast up dust upon their heads**, they shall wallow themselves in the ashes: And they shall make themselves utterly bald for thee, and gird them with sackcloth, and **they shall weep for thee with bitterness of heart and bitter wailing**. And in their wailing they shall take up a lamentation for thee, and lament over thee, saying, **What city is like Tyre, like the destroyed in the midst of the sea?** When thy wares went forth out of the seas, thou filledst many people; **thou didst enrich the kings of the earth** with the multitude of thy riches and of thy merchandise."

**Compare with Revelation 18:19**: "And **they cast dust on their heads**, and cried, weeping and wailing, saying, Alas, alas, that great city, wherein were made rich all that had ships in the sea by reason of her costliness! for in one hour is she made desolate."

**Compare with Revelation 18:18**: "And cried when they saw the smoke of her burning, saying, **What city is like unto this great city!**"

**Compare with Revelation 18:3**: "...the kings of the earth...and **the merchants of the earth are waxed rich** through the abundance of her delicacies."

**The parallels are unmistakable**—Tyre is a type of end-time Babylon.

### Tyre Destroyed Permanently

**Ezekiel 27:34-36**: "In the time when thou shalt be broken by the seas in the depths of the waters thy merchandise and all thy company in the midst of thee shall fall. All the inhabitants of the isles shall be astonished at thee, and their kings shall be sore afraid, they shall be troubled in their countenance. The merchants among the people shall hiss at thee; **thou shalt be a terror, and never shalt be any more**."

**Compare with Revelation 18:21**: "**Thus with violence shall that great city Babylon be thrown down, and shall be found no more at all**."

**Both Tyre and Babylon**:
- Destroyed violently
- Destroyed permanently ("never shalt be any more")
- Merchants and kings mourn
- Complete astonishment at the fall

## Part 7: The Prince of Tyre—The Human Leader

### Pride and Blasphemy

**Ezekiel 28:1-2**: "The word of the LORD came again unto me, saying, Son of man, say unto the **prince of Tyre**, Thus saith the Lord GOD; Because **thine heart is lifted up, and thou hast said, I am a God, I sit in the seat of God, in the midst of the seas**; yet **thou art a man, and not God**, though thou set thine heart as the heart of God."

**The prince of Tyre**:
1. **Heart lifted up** (pride)
2. **Said "I am a God"** (blasphemy, claiming divinity)
3. **"I sit in the seat of God"** (claiming God's throne)
4. **"In the midst of the seas"** (among the peoples—see Revelation 17:15)
5. **Yet "thou art a man, and not God"** (human, not divine)

**This parallels the papacy**:

**2 Thessalonians 2:4**: "who opposes and exalts himself above all that is called God or that is worshiped, so that **he sits as God in the temple of God, showing himself that he is God**."

**Revelation 17:15**: "And he saith unto me, The waters which thou sawest, where the whore sitteth, are **peoples, and multitudes, and nations, and tongues**."

**The prince of Tyre** = a human leader who claims to be God = the pope

### Wisdom, Riches, and Pride

**Ezekiel 28:3-6**: "Behold, **thou art wiser than Daniel**; there is no secret that they can hide from thee: With thy wisdom and with thine understanding **thou hast gotten thee riches, and hast gotten gold and silver into thy treasures**: By thy great wisdom and by thy traffick hast thou increased thy riches, and **thine heart is lifted up because of thy riches**: Therefore thus saith the Lord GOD; Because thou hast set thine heart as the heart of God..."

**Characteristics**:
1. Claims to be wiser than Daniel (proud of intellect)
2. Gotten riches (gold and silver—like Babylon in Revelation 18)
3. Increased riches through trade (trafficking, like Babylon)
4. Heart lifted up (pride because of wealth)
5. Set heart as God's heart (thinks he equals God)

**This describes the papacy**:
- Claims to have divine wisdom (infallibility)
- Immense wealth (Vatican's riches)
- Trade and commerce (indulgences, merchandise)
- Pride because of riches
- Claims to stand in the place of God

### Judgment Coming

**Ezekiel 28:7-10**: "Behold, therefore I will bring strangers upon thee, the terrible of the nations: and they shall draw their swords against the beauty of thy wisdom, and they shall defile thy brightness. They shall bring thee down to the pit, and thou shalt die the deaths of them that are slain in the midst of the seas. **Wilt thou yet say before him that slayeth thee, I am God? but thou shalt be a man, and no God**, in the hand of him that slayeth thee. Thou shalt die the deaths of the uncircumcised by the hand of strangers: for I have spoken it, saith the Lord GOD."

**God's judgment**:
- Strangers will attack (enemies will come)
- Die in the midst of the seas (among the peoples)
- **"Wilt thou yet say...I am God?"** (will you still claim divinity when you're dying?)
- **"Thou shalt be a man, and no God"** (your mortality will be exposed)

**The pope claims to be "God on earth"—but when judgment comes, his humanity and mortality will be revealed.**

## Part 8: The King of Tyre—Satan Behind the System

### Perfect in Beauty—Until Iniquity Was Found

**Ezekiel 28:11-15**: "Moreover the word of the LORD came unto me, saying, Son of man, take up a lamentation upon the **king of Tyre**, and say unto him, Thus saith the Lord GOD; Thou sealest up the sum, **full of wisdom, and perfect in beauty**. **Thou hast been in Eden the garden of God**; every precious stone was thy covering, the sardius, topaz, and the diamond, the beryl, the onyx, and the jasper, the sapphire, the emerald, and the carbuncle, and gold: the workmanship of thy tabrets and of thy pipes was prepared in thee **in the day that thou wast created**. **Thou art the anointed cherub that covereth**; and I have set thee so: **thou wast upon the holy mountain of God**; thou hast walked up and down in the midst of the stones of fire. **Thou wast perfect in thy ways from the day that thou wast created, till iniquity was found in thee**."

**The king of Tyre** (distinct from the "prince of Tyre"):
1. **Full of wisdom** (originally created wise)
2. **Perfect in beauty** (originally glorious)
3. **In Eden the garden of God** (no human king was in Eden—this is Satan)
4. **Covered with precious stones** (angelic glory)
5. **"In the day thou wast created"** (created being—not eternal)
6. **"Anointed cherub that covereth"** (highest rank of angel, covering God's throne)
7. **"Upon the holy mountain of God"** (in heaven)
8. **"Walked...in the midst of the stones of fire"** (in God's presence)
9. **"Perfect...until iniquity was found"** (originally sinless, then rebelled)

**This cannot be a human king—this is Satan, Lucifer, the fallen angel.**

**The "king" is the power behind the "prince"**—Satan is the power behind the papal system.

### Pride Led to the Fall

**Ezekiel 28:16-19**: "By the multitude of thy merchandise they have filled the midst of thee with violence, and thou hast sinned: therefore I will cast thee as profane out of the mountain of God: and I will destroy thee, O covering cherub, from the midst of the stones of fire. **Thine heart was lifted up because of thy beauty, thou hast corrupted thy wisdom by reason of thy brightness**: I will cast thee to the ground, I will lay thee before kings, that they may behold thee. Thou hast defiled thy sanctuaries by the multitude of thine iniquities, by the iniquity of thy traffick; therefore will I bring forth a fire from the midst of thee, it shall devour thee, and **I will bring thee to ashes upon the earth** in the sight of all them that behold thee. All they that know thee among the people shall be astonished at thee: **thou shalt be a terror, and never shalt thou be any more**."

**Satan's sin**:
1. **Heart lifted up because of beauty** (pride in his appearance)
2. **Corrupted wisdom by brightness** (intelligence turned to evil)
3. Merchandise and trafficking (deceiving, buying and selling souls)
4. Violence (destroying God's people)

**Satan's fate**:
1. Cast out of heaven (already happened—Revelation 12:7-9)
2. Cast to the ground (already happened—Revelation 12:9)
3. Laid before kings (exposed before all)
4. **Brought to ashes upon the earth** (final destruction)
5. **Never shalt be any more** (eternal annihilation)

**Satan will be destroyed—and Babylon's system with him.**

## Part 9: The Gospel Call—Salvation by Grace Through Faith

### Jesus Is the Only Way

**John 14:6**: "Jesus said to him, '**I am the way, the truth, and the life. No one comes to the Father except through Me**.'"

**Acts 4:12**: "**Nor is there salvation in any other**, for there is **no other name under heaven given among men by which we must be saved**."

**Salvation is found**:
- Only in Jesus (not in Mary, not in saints, not in the pope)
- Only through His name
- Not through human mediators

**Babylon teaches salvation through**:
- The church
- The pope
- Priests
- Sacraments
- Mary and the saints

**But Scripture is clear: salvation is through Jesus Christ alone.**

### Confession and Cleansing

**1 John 1:9**: "If we **confess our sins**, He is faithful and just to **forgive us our sins, and to cleanse us from all unrighteousness**."

**God's promise**:
- Confess (acknowledge, admit, turn from sin)
- He forgives (removes guilt)
- He cleanses (removes defilement)

**You don't confess to a priest—you confess to Jesus**, the only mediator (1 Timothy 2:5).

### New Creation in Christ

**2 Corinthians 5:17**: "Therefore if any man be in Christ, **he is a new creature: old things are passed away; behold, all things are become new**."

**Ezekiel 36:26-27**: "**I will give you a new heart and put a new spirit within you**; I will take the heart of stone out of your flesh and give you a heart of flesh. **I will put My Spirit within you and cause you to walk in My statutes**, and you will keep My judgments and do them."

**True conversion**:
- New heart (new desires, new nature)
- New spirit (Holy Spirit dwelling within)
- Empowered to obey (God causes us to walk in His statutes)

**This is not human effort—this is God's transforming grace.**

## Part 10: Faith and Works—Not Either/Or, But Both

### The Just Shall Live by Faith

**Galatians 3:11**: "But that **no one is justified by the law** in the sight of God is evident, for '**the just shall live by faith**.'"

**Romans 3:31**: "**Do we then make void the law through faith? Certainly not! On the contrary, we establish the law**."

**Faith does not abolish the law—it establishes it.**

**We are not justified BY keeping the law (that's legalism).**
**We are justified BY FAITH—and faith leads to keeping the law (that's grace).**

### Saved by Grace Through Faith

**Ephesians 2:8-10**: "For **by grace are ye saved through faith**; and that not of yourselves: **it is the gift of God**: Not of works, lest any man should boast. For **we are his workmanship, created in Christ Jesus unto good works**, which God hath before ordained that we should walk in them."

**The Gospel**:
1. **Saved by grace** (unmerited favor)
2. **Through faith** (trusting in Christ)
3. **Gift of God** (not earned)
4. **Not of works** (can't boast)
5. **Created unto good works** (saved FOR good works, not BY good works)

**Salvation is a gift—but the gift includes transformation that produces obedience.**

### Faith Without Works Is Dead

**James 2:17-20**: "Thus also **faith by itself, if it does not have works, is dead**. But someone will say, 'You have faith, and I have works.' **Show me your faith without your works, and I will show you my faith by my works**. You believe that there is one God. You do well. **Even the demons believe--and tremble!** But do you want to know, O foolish man, that **faith without works is dead**?"

**True faith produces works**:
- Faith alone (without works) = dead faith
- Demons believe (intellectual assent) but don't obey = not saving faith
- True faith = trust that results in obedience

**You can't separate faith and works—they are two sides of the same coin.**

## Part 11: Knowing Christ Means Keeping His Commandments

### Jesus Our Advocate
