- One week (7 years) remained: 27-34 CE

**In Part 2**, we will examine:
1. **"Messiah shall be cut off"** - Jesus' crucifixion in 31 CE (midst of the 70th week)
2. **The destruction of Jerusalem** - Fulfilled in 70 CE

## Part 1: "Messiah Shall Be Cut Off" - The Crucifixion Foretold

### Daniel's Prophecy: The Timing

> **Daniel 9:26** - "And after threescore and two weeks [after the 69 weeks = 27 CE] shall Messiah be cut off, but not for Himself: and the people of the prince that shall come shall destroy the city and the sanctuary; and the end thereof shall be with a flood, and unto the end of the war desolations are determined."

**Key phrase**: **"Messiah shall be cut off"**

**What does "cut off" mean?**
- Hebrew: *karath* (כָּרַת) = to cut down, destroy, kill, execute
- Used throughout Old Testament for violent death, execution, being killed
- **This is a prophecy of the Messiah's death**

---

**Timing**:
- **"After threescore and two weeks"** = After the 69 weeks (483 years from 457 BCE)
- The 69 weeks ended at Jesus' baptism in **27 CE**
- One week (7 years) remained: **27-34 CE**

> **Daniel 9:27** - "And He shall confirm the covenant with many for one week: and **in the midst of the week** He shall cause the sacrifice and the oblation to cease..."

**"In the midst of the week"** = Middle of the final 7 years

**The math**:
- 69 weeks ended: 27 CE (Jesus' baptism)
- Final week: 27-34 CE
- **Midst of the week**: 27 CE + 3.5 years = **31 CE**

**Prophecy**: The Messiah would be "cut off" (killed) in **31 CE**, exactly **3.5 years after His baptism**.

---

### "But Not for Himself" - Died for Others

> **Daniel 9:26b** - "...Messiah be cut off, but **not for Himself**..."

**Key phrase**: **"not for Himself"**

**What this means**: The Messiah would die, but **not for His own sins**—He would die for **others**.

**Isaiah's prophecy confirms this** (written 700 years before Jesus):

> **Isaiah 53:4-6** - "Surely He hath borne **our griefs**, and carried **our sorrows**: yet we did esteem Him stricken, smitten of God, and afflicted. But He was wounded for **our transgressions**, He was bruised for **our iniquities**: the chastisement of **our peace** was upon Him; and with His stripes **we are healed**. All we like sheep have gone astray; we have turned every one to his own way; and the LORD hath laid on Him **the iniquity of us all**."

**Notice the pronouns**:
- **He** (Messiah) suffered
- **Our** griefs, **our** sorrows, **our** transgressions, **our** iniquities
- **We** are healed by **His** stripes
- **Our** iniquity laid on **Him**

**The substitutionary sacrifice**: Jesus took our punishment so we could have His righteousness.

---

### Why Did Jesus Have to Die?

> **Note**: This foundational section briefly recaps Part 1's key gospel points so that Part 2 stands complete on its own. For the full treatment, see Part 1, Section 1.

> **Romans 3:10** - "As it is written, There is none righteous, no, not one"

> **Romans 3:23** - "For all have sinned, and come short of the glory of God"

> **Romans 6:23** - "For the wages of sin is death; but the gift of God is eternal life through Jesus Christ our Lord."

**The equation**:
1. All humans are sinners
2. The penalty for sin is death
3. **We all deserve to die**

---

**The Solution: Jesus Died in Our Place**

> **John 8:24** - "Therefore I said to you that you will die in your sins; for if you do not believe that I am He, you will die in your sins."

> **John 14:6** - "Jesus said to him, 'I am the way, the truth, and the life. No one comes to the Father except through Me.'"

**Jesus is the only way of salvation**:
- He lived a sinless life (qualified to be our substitute)
- He died in our place (paid the penalty we deserved)
- He rose from the dead (proved He conquered sin and death)

**This is why the Messiah had to be "cut off, but not for Himself"**—He died for **us**.

## Part 2: Isaiah 53 - The Suffering Servant Prophecy

### Written 700 Years Before Jesus

**Isaiah 53** is one of the most remarkable prophecies in Scripture. Written approximately **700 BCE**, it describes the Messiah's suffering, death, and purpose with stunning detail—**700 years before Jesus was crucified**.

**Key question**: Who is Isaiah 53 talking about? Let's let the prophecy speak for itself.

---

### Despised and Rejected

> **Isaiah 53:3** - "He is despised and rejected of men; a Man of sorrows, and acquainted with grief: and we hid as it were our faces from him; He was despised, and we esteemed Him not."

**Prophecy**: The Messiah would be:
- Despised and rejected by His own people
- A man of sorrows and grief
- Not esteemed (not valued, not honored)

**Fulfillment**:
- The Jewish religious leaders rejected Jesus (John 1:11 - "He came to His own, and His own did not receive Him")
- The crowds who praised Him on Palm Sunday crucified Him five days later
- His own disciple (Judas) betrayed Him for 30 pieces of silver
- Even His closest followers abandoned Him when arrested (Mark 14:50)

---

### Wounded for Our Transgressions

> **Isaiah 53:4-5** - "Surely He hath borne our griefs, and carried our sorrows: yet we did esteem Him stricken, smitten of God, and afflicted. But He was wounded for our transgressions, He was bruised for our iniquities: the chastisement of our peace was upon Him; and with His stripes we are healed."

**Prophecy**:
1. **"Borne our griefs and carried our sorrows"** = He took our suffering upon Himself
2. **"Wounded for our transgressions"** = Injured because of our sins
3. **"Bruised for our iniquities"** = Crushed for our wickedness
4. **"Chastisement of our peace"** = Punishment that brings us peace with God
5. **"With His stripes we are healed"** = His physical suffering brings our spiritual healing

**Fulfillment**: Jesus was:
- Severely scourged (John 19:1 — the Roman flogging was notoriously brutal; Jewish law limited flogging to 40 stripes minus one: 2 Corinthians 11:24)
- Crowned with thorns (Matthew 27:29)
- Nailed to a cross (John 20:25)
- Pierced in His side (John 19:34)

**All this suffering was "for us"—not for anything He did wrong.**

---

### All We Like Sheep Have Gone Astray

> **Isaiah 53:6** - "All we like sheep have gone astray; we have turned every one to his own way; and the LORD hath laid on Him the iniquity of us all."

**This verse summarizes the entire gospel**:

1. **"All we like sheep have gone astray"** = Every human is a sinner, wandering from God
2. **"Every one to his own way"** = We chose rebellion, selfishness, independence from God
3. **"The LORD hath laid on Him the iniquity of us all"** = God placed our sin on Jesus

**The great exchange**:
- Our sin → placed on Jesus
- Jesus' righteousness → given to us
- We deserve death → Jesus took our death
- Jesus deserved glory → we receive His reward

**This is the gospel.**

---

### Silent Before His Accusers

> **Isaiah 53:7** - "He was oppressed, and He was afflicted, yet He opened not His mouth: He is brought as a lamb to the slaughter, and as a sheep before her shearers is dumb, so He openeth not His mouth."

**Prophecy**: The Messiah would:
- Be oppressed and afflicted
- Not defend Himself (opened not His mouth)
- Go like a lamb to slaughter (willingly, silently)

**Fulfillment**:

> **Matthew 27:12-14** - "And while He was being accused by the chief priests and elders, He answered nothing. Then Pilate said to Him, 'Do You not hear how many things they testify against You?' But He answered him not one word, so that the governor marveled greatly."

> **Mark 15:3-5** - "And the chief priests accused Him of many things, but He answered nothing. Then Pilate asked Him again, saying, 'Do You answer nothing? See how many things they testify against You!' But Jesus still answered nothing, so that Pilate marveled."

**Jesus fulfilled this prophecy exactly**: He remained silent before His accusers, like a lamb led to slaughter.

---

### Cut Off Out of the Land of the Living

> **Isaiah 53:8** - "He was taken from prison and from judgment: and who shall declare His generation? for **He was cut off out of the land of the living**: for the transgression of my people was He stricken."

**Key phrase**: **"cut off out of the land of the living"** = killed, executed

**Same language as Daniel 9:26**: "Messiah shall be **cut off**"

**Why was He cut off?** "For the transgression of **my people** was He stricken" = He died for **our** sins, not His own.

---

### Made His Grave with the Wicked and the Rich

> **Isaiah 53:9** - "And He made His grave with the wicked, and with the rich in His death; because He had done no violence, neither was any deceit in His mouth."

**Prophecy**: The Messiah would be buried:
- With the wicked (criminals)
- With the rich
- Despite being innocent (no violence, no deceit)

**Fulfillment**:
- Jesus was crucified between **two thieves** (wicked criminals) - Matthew 27:38
- Jesus was buried in the tomb of **Joseph of Arimathea**, a **rich man** - Matthew 27:57-60

**This detail is so specific, it could not be coincidence**:
- Died with the wicked (criminals on crosses beside Him)
- Buried with the rich (Joseph's unused tomb)
- Both prophecies fulfilled in one day

---

### An Offering for Sin

> **Isaiah 53:10** - "Yet it pleased the LORD to bruise Him; He hath put Him to grief: when thou shalt make His soul an offering for sin, He shall see His seed, He shall prolong His days, and the pleasure of the LORD shall prosper in His hand."

**Key phrase**: **"offering for sin"**

**The sacrificial system pointed to Jesus**:
- Old Testament: Animals sacrificed for sin (temporary covering)
- New Testament: Jesus sacrificed for sin (permanent payment)

> **Hebrews 10:4** - "For it is not possible that the blood of bulls and goats could take away sins."

> **Hebrews 10:10** - "By that will we have been sanctified through the offering of the body of Jesus Christ once for all."

**Jesus is the ultimate sacrifice—the Lamb of God who takes away the sin of the world (John 1:29).**

---

### He Shall Bear Their Iniquities

> **Isaiah 53:11** - "He shall see of the travail of His soul, and shall be satisfied: by His knowledge shall My righteous servant justify many; for He shall bear their iniquities."

**Key phrase**: **"He shall bear their iniquities"**

**What this means**: Jesus took our sins upon Himself. He carried the weight of humanity's guilt and shame.

> **2 Corinthians 5:21** - "For He made Him who knew no sin to be sin for us, that we might become the righteousness of God in Him."

**The great exchange again**:
- Jesus (sinless) became sin for us
- We (sinful) become righteous through Him

---

### Summary: Isaiah 53 Fulfilled in Jesus

**Every detail of Isaiah 53 was fulfilled in Jesus**:

✓ Despised and rejected (verse 3)  
✓ Wounded for our transgressions (verse 5)  
✓ Silent before accusers (verse 7)  
✓ Cut off out of the land of the living (verse 8)  
✓ Buried with the wicked and the rich (verse 9)  
✓ Made an offering for sin (verse 10)  
✓ Bore our iniquities (verse 11)

**This prophecy was written 700 years before Jesus was born. The odds of one person fulfilling all these details by chance are astronomically impossible.**

**Conclusion**: Jesus is the Messiah Isaiah prophesied about.

## Part 3: "In the Midst of the Week" - The Crucifixion (31 CE)

### The Final Week: 27-34 CE

> **Daniel 9:27** - "And He shall confirm the covenant with many for one week: and **in the midst of the week** He shall cause the sacrifice and the oblation to cease, and for the overspreading of abominations He shall make it desolate, even until the consummation, and that determined shall be poured upon the desolate."

**The timeline**:
- 69 weeks ended: **27 CE** (Jesus' baptism, "Messiah the Prince")
- One week remained: **27-34 CE** (7 years)
- **Midst of the week**: 27 + 3.5 years = **31 CE**

**Prophecy**: "In the midst of the week He shall cause the sacrifice and the oblation to cease"

**What this means**:
1. **Timing**: The Messiah would die in **31 CE** (middle of the final week)
2. **Effect**: His death would **end the sacrificial system** (no more need for animal sacrifices)

---

### The Veil Torn in Two: Sacrifices Ended

**Fulfillment at the moment of Jesus' death**:

> **Mark 15:37-38** - "And Jesus cried out with a loud voice, and breathed His last. Then **the veil of the temple was torn in two from top to bottom**."

**What was the veil?**
- A massive curtain separating the Holy Place from the Most Holy Place in the temple
- Only the high priest could pass through, once per year (Day of Atonement)
- Represented the barrier between God and sinful humanity

**Why did it tear?**
- **From top to bottom** = God tore it (not humans from bottom to top)
- Jesus' death removed the barrier between God and man
- No more need for earthly high priests or animal sacrifices
- **The sacrificial system ended** exactly as Daniel prophesied

> **Hebrews 10:19-20** - "Therefore, brethren, having boldness to enter the Holiest by the blood of Jesus, by a new and living way which He consecrated for us, through the veil, that is, His flesh"

**Jesus' body ("His flesh") was the veil**. When His body was torn on the cross, the temple veil tore—symbolizing access to God now open through Jesus' sacrifice.

---

### "Your House Is Left Desolate"

> **Daniel 9:27b** - "...and for the overspreading of abominations He shall make it desolate..."

> **Matthew 23:37-38** - "O Jerusalem, Jerusalem, thou that killest the prophets, and stonest them which are sent unto thee, how often would I have gathered thy children together, even as a hen gathereth her chickens under her wings, and ye would not! Behold, **your house is left unto you desolate**."

**Jesus pronounced judgment on the temple**: "Your house is left unto you desolate"

**When the veil tore, God's presence left the temple**:
- No more Shekinah glory
- No more divine presence between the cherubim
- The temple became an empty building—spiritually desolate

**Historical fulfillment**: In **70 CE** (39 years later), the Romans destroyed Jerusalem and the temple, exactly as Daniel and Jesus prophesied.

## Part 4: Psalm 22 - The Crucifixion in Detail (1,000 Years Before)

**Psalm 22** was written by King David approximately **1000 BCE**—**1,000 years before crucifixion was even invented as a method of execution**. Yet it describes Jesus' crucifixion with stunning precision.

### "My God, My God, Why Have You Forsaken Me?"

> **Psalm 22:1** - "My God, My God, why have You forsaken Me? Why are You so far from helping Me, and from the words of My groaning?"

**Fulfillment**:

> **Matthew 27:46** - "And about the ninth hour Jesus cried out with a loud voice, saying, **Eli, Eli, lama sabachthani?** that is, **My God, My God, why have You forsaken Me?**"

**Jesus quoted Psalm 22:1 from the cross**—fulfilling the prophecy and identifying Himself as the suffering Messiah David wrote about.

**Why did God forsake Jesus?**
- Jesus was bearing the sins of the world (Isaiah 53:6)
- God's holiness cannot look upon sin (Habakkuk 1:13)
- Jesus experienced separation from the Father so we would never have to

---

### Mocked and Ridiculed

> **Psalm 22:6-8** - "But I am a worm, and no man; a reproach of men, and despised by the people. All those who see Me ridicule Me; they shoot out the lip, they shake the head, saying, 'He trusted in the Lord, let Him rescue Him; let Him deliver Him, since He delights in Him!'"

**Prophecy**: The Messiah would be:
- Mocked and ridiculed
- People would shake their heads
- They would say "He trusted God, let God rescue Him"

**Fulfillment**:

> **Matthew 27:42-43** - "'He saved others; Himself He cannot save. If He is the King of Israel, let Him now come down from the cross, and we will believe Him. He trusted in God; let Him deliver Him now if He will have Him; for He said, "I am the Son of God."'"

**The mockers quoted Psalm 22 almost word-for-word without realizing they were fulfilling prophecy!**

---

### Blood and Water: "Poured Out Like Water"

> **Psalm 22:14** - "I am poured out like water, and all My bones are out of joint; My heart is like wax; it has melted within Me."

**Prophecy**: "Poured out like water" = blood and bodily fluids flowing

**Fulfillment**:

> **John 19:34** - "But one of the soldiers pierced His side with a spear, and immediately **blood and water came out**."

**Medical fact**: When a person dies by crucifixion, blood and plasma separate. The "water" was plasma (clear fluid). This proves Jesus was truly dead when pierced.

**"Heart like wax... melted"** = some medical historians propose this as a reference to cardiac rupture — one proposed cause of death in crucifixion victims (Edwards et al., *Journal of the American Medical Association*, 1986). Whether the exact mechanism was cardiac rupture or circulatory collapse, the suffering described matches crucifixion physiology.

**Psalm 22 predicted even the physical details.**

---

### "I Thirst"

> **Psalm 22:15** - "My strength is dried up like a potsherd, and My tongue clings to My jaws; You have brought Me to the dust of death."

**Prophecy**: Extreme thirst, tongue clinging to mouth

**Fulfillment**:

> **John 19:28** - "After this, Jesus, knowing that all things were now accomplished, that the Scripture might be fulfilled, said, **'I thirst!'**"

**Jesus said "I thirst" to fulfill Scripture**—even in His dying moments, He was conscious of fulfilling prophecy.

---

### Pierced Hands and Feet

> **Psalm 22:16** - "For dogs have surrounded Me; the congregation of the wicked has enclosed Me. **They pierced My hands and My feet**"

**This is the most remarkable verse in Psalm 22**:

**"They pierced My hands and My feet"** = **crucifixion**

**Historical fact**: Crucifixion was **not invented until the 6th century BCE** (600s BCE), about **400 years after David wrote Psalm 22** (1000 BCE). The Romans perfected it as an execution method in the 1st century CE.

**David described a form of execution that didn't exist yet—and it was fulfilled exactly when Jesus was crucified.**

**Fulfillment**:

> **John 20:25** - "The other disciples therefore said to him, 'We have seen the Lord.' So he said to them, 'Unless I see in His hands **the print of the nails**, and put my finger into **the print of the nails**, and put my hand into His side, I will not believe.'"

**Jesus' hands and feet were pierced with nails—exactly as Psalm 22:16 prophesied 1,000 years earlier.**

---

### Bones Not Broken

> **Psalm 22:17** - "I can count all My bones. They look and stare at Me."

**Prophecy**: The Messiah's bones would remain intact (not broken)

**This connects to another prophecy**:

> **Psalm 34:20** - "He guards all his bones; not one of them is broken."

**Fulfillment**:

> **John 19:32-33** - "Then the soldiers came and broke the legs of the first and of the other who was crucified with Him. But when they came to Jesus and saw that He was already dead, **they did not break His legs**."

**Roman custom**: Break the legs of crucifixion victims to speed death (they can't push up to breathe, suffocate faster)

**Jesus died before the soldiers could break His legs—fulfilling the prophecy that no bones would be broken.**

---

### Divided His Garments, Cast Lots

> **Psalm 22:18** - "They divide My garments among them, and for My clothing they cast lots."

**Prophecy**: 
- Garments divided
- Lots cast for clothing

**Fulfillment**:

> **Matthew 27:35** - "Then they crucified Him, and **divided His garments, casting lots**, that it might be fulfilled which was spoken by the prophet: 'They divided My garments among them, and for My clothing they cast lots.'"

> **John 19:23-24** - "Then the soldiers, when they had crucified Jesus, took His garments and made four parts, to each soldier a part, and also the tunic. Now the tunic was without seam, woven from the top in one piece. They said therefore among themselves, 'Let us not tear it, but cast lots for it, whose it shall be,' that the Scripture might be fulfilled which says: 'They divided My garments among them, and for My clothing they cast lots.' Therefore the soldiers did these things."

**Roman soldiers fulfilled prophecy without knowing it**: They divided His outer garments into four parts (one for each soldier) and gambled for His seamless tunic.

---

### Summary: Psalm 22 - Impossible to Fulfill by Chance

**Psalm 22 predicted**:
✓ "My God, My God, why have You forsaken Me?" (verse 1) - Jesus' exact words on the cross  
✓ Mocked and ridiculed (verses 6-8) - Fulfilled by the crowds  
✓ Poured out like water (verse 14) - Blood and water from His side  
✓ Extreme thirst (verse 15) - "I thirst"  
✓ **Pierced hands and feet (verse 16)** - Crucifixion (described 1,000 years before it was invented!)  
✓ Bones not broken (verse 17) - Legs not broken  
✓ Garments divided, lots cast (verse 18) - Soldiers gambling for His clothes

**Written 1,000 years before Jesus was born. Fulfilled in every detail.**

**Question**: Could Jesus have arranged to fulfill these prophecies?

**Answer**: No. He couldn't control:
- What the mockers would say
- Whether soldiers would pierce Him
- Whether they would break His bones
- What they would do with His clothes

**These were actions of others—yet perfectly fulfilled prophecy.**

**Conclusion**: Jesus is the Messiah Psalm 22 prophesied about.

## Part 5: The Resurrection - Victory Over Death

### Prophecies of the Resurrection

**The Old Testament predicted the Messiah would not only die, but also rise from the dead.**

> **Psalm 16:10** - "For You will not leave my soul in Sheol, nor will You allow Your Holy One to see corruption."

**Prophecy**:
- **"Not leave my soul in Sheol"** = Not remain in the grave
- **"Not allow Your Holy One to see corruption"** = Body will not decay

**This prophecy requires resurrection within 3 days** (before the body begins to decompose).

---

> **Hosea 6:2** - "After two days will He revive us: in the third day He will raise us up, and we shall live in His sight."

**Prophecy**: Resurrection **on the third day**

**Fulfillment**: Jesus rose on the third day (Friday crucifixion, Sunday resurrection).

---

### The Resurrection Fulfilled

> **Matthew 28:1-6** - "Now after the Sabbath, as the first day of the week began to dawn, Mary Magdalene and the other Mary came to see the tomb. And behold, there was a great earthquake; for an angel of the Lord descended from heaven, and came and rolled back the stone from the door, and sat on it. His countenance was like lightning, and his clothing as white as snow. And the guards shook for fear of him, and became like dead men. But the angel answered and said to the women, 'Do not be afraid, for I know that you seek Jesus who was crucified. **He is not here; for He is risen, as He said. Come, see the place where the Lord lay.**'"

**The angel's message**:
1. **"He is not here"** = The tomb is empty
2. **"He is risen"** = Jesus is alive
3. **"As He said"** = Exactly as He predicted (Mark 8:31, 9:31, 10:34)

---

### Jesus Appeared to His Disciples

> **Luke 24:36-43** - "Now as they said these things, Jesus Himself stood in the midst of them, and said to them, 'Peace to you.' But they were terrified and frightened, and supposed they had seen a spirit. And He said to them, 'Why are you troubled? And why do doubts arise in your hearts? Behold My hands and My feet, that it is I Myself. **Handle Me and see, for a spirit does not have flesh and bones as you see I have.**' When He had said this, He showed them His hands and His feet. But while they still did not believe for joy, and marveled, He said to them, 'Have you any food here?' So they gave Him a piece of a broiled fish and some honeycomb. And He took it and ate in their presence."

**Jesus proved His resurrection was physical**:
1. **"Handle Me and see"** = Touch Me, I'm real
2. **"Flesh and bones"** = Physical body, not a ghost
3. **Showed wounds** = Same body that was crucified
4. **Ate food** = Physical bodies eat; spirits don't

**The resurrection was bodily, physical, and real.**

---

### Ascension: "Led Captivity Captive"

> **Psalm 68:18** - "Thou hast ascended on high, Thou hast led captivity captive: Thou hast received gifts for men; yea, for the rebellious also, that the LORD God might dwell among them."

**Prophecy**: The Messiah would:
- Ascend on high
- Lead captivity captive (free the captives)
- Receive gifts for men

**Fulfillment**:

> **Acts 1:9-11** - "Now when He had spoken these things, while they watched, He was taken up, and a cloud received Him out of their sight. And while they looked steadfastly toward heaven as He went up, behold, two men stood by them in white apparel, who also said, 'Men of Galilee, why do you stand gazing up into heaven? This same Jesus, who was taken up from you into heaven, will so come in like manner as you saw Him go into heaven.'"

**Jesus ascended to heaven 40 days after His resurrection—fulfilling Psalm 68:18.**

---

> **Revelation 1:18** - "I am He that liveth, and was dead; and, behold, **I am alive for evermore**, Amen; and **have the keys of hell and of death**."

**Jesus' declaration**:
1. **"Was dead"** = He truly died on the cross
2. **"Alive for evermore"** = Resurrected, never to die again
3. **"Keys of hell and death"** = Conquered death, has authority over the grave

**Because Jesus rose from the dead, we know**:
- Death is not the end
- Jesus has power over death
- We will also be resurrected (1 Corinthians 15:20-23)

## Part 6: Jesus Opened Their Understanding

### The Old Testament Testified of Jesus

> **John 5:39** - "You search the Scriptures, for in them you think you have eternal life; and **these are they which testify of Me**."

**Jesus said the Old Testament Scriptures testify of Him**—they point to Him as the Messiah.

---

> **Luke 24:44-45** - "Then He said to them, 'These are the words which I spoke to you while I was still with you, that all things must be fulfilled which were written in **the Law of Moses and the Prophets and the Psalms** concerning Me.' And **He opened their understanding, that they might comprehend the Scriptures**."

**After His resurrection, Jesus explained how the entire Old Testament pointed to Him**:

1. **Law of Moses** = The sacrificial system (Leviticus) foreshadowed Jesus' sacrifice
2. **The Prophets** = Isaiah 53, Daniel 9, and others prophesied His coming
3. **The Psalms** = Psalm 22, 16, 68, 110, and others described His suffering and victory

**Jesus "opened their understanding"**—they saw how prophecy pointed to Him.

---

### From Abraham to Jesus: The Promised Seed

> **Genesis 22:15-18** - "Then the Angel of the Lord called to Abraham a second time out of heaven, and said: 'By Myself I have sworn, says the Lord, because you have done this thing, and have not withheld your son, your only son—blessing I will bless you, and multiplying I will multiply your descendants as the stars of the heaven and as the sand which is on the seashore; and your descendants shall possess the gate of their enemies. **In your seed all the nations of the earth shall be blessed**, because you have obeyed My voice.'"

**God's promise to Abraham**: "In your seed all the nations of the earth shall be blessed"

**Who is the "seed"?**

> **Galatians 3:16** - "Now to Abraham and his Seed were the promises made. He does not say, 'And to seeds,' as of many, but as of one, **'And to your Seed,' who is Christ**."

**The "seed" is Jesus Christ**—through Him, all nations are blessed (salvation offered to the whole world).

---

### Born of a Virgin

> **Isaiah 7:14** - "Therefore the Lord himself shall give you a sign; Behold, **a virgin shall conceive, and bear a son**, and shall call His name Immanuel."

**But in Acts 7:56, Jesus is STANDING.**

**Why?**

**Possible reasons Jesus stood at this pivotal moment**:
1. **Stephen was the first martyr**—Jesus stood to honor him
2. **The 70 weeks were ending**—God's special probation for the Jewish nation was concluding
3. **The gospel was about to go to the Gentiles**—Jesus stood as Judge, marking the transition

## Part 11: The Nature of Jesus - God in Flesh

**One of the most misunderstood doctrines**: How could Jesus be both God and man?

**Jesus is fully God**:

**"The Word" (Jesus) was God from eternity, then became flesh (human).**

---

**Jesus is fully man**:

> **Romans 1:3** - "concerning His Son Jesus Christ our Lord, who **was born of the seed of David according to the flesh**"

**Jesus took human nature**—He was born as a human, lived as a human, died as a human.

### Why Did Jesus Have to Become Human?

> **Hebrews 2:14** - "Forasmuch then as the children are partakers of flesh and blood, **He also Himself likewise took part of the same**; that through death he might destroy him that had the power of death, that is, the devil"

**Reason #1**: To die in our place  
- Angels can't die for humans
- God (in His divine nature) cannot die
- **Jesus became human so He could die as our substitute**

---

> **Hebrews 2:18** - "For in that He Himself has suffered, being tempted, He is able to aid those who are tempted."

> **Hebrews 4:15** - "For we do not have a High Priest who cannot sympathize with our weaknesses, but **was in all points tempted as we are, yet without sin**."

**Reason #2**: To be tempted in every way we are, yet remain sinless  
- Jesus experienced human temptation
- He knows what we go through
- He can sympathize with our struggles
- **Yet He never sinned**—proving it's possible to overcome through God's power

### The Glory of God Revealed

> **John 1:14** - "And the Word became flesh and dwelt among us, and **we beheld His glory**, the glory as of the only begotten of the Father, full of grace and truth."

> **Exodus 33:18** - "And [Moses] said, 'Please, show me Your glory.'"

**God's glory = His character**: merciful, gracious, longsuffering, full of goodness and truth, forgiving.

**Jesus revealed God's glory (character) perfectly**:

**What Tacitus confirms**:

4. **Christianity spread rapidly** - "broke out" despite persecution

**Even a hostile, non-Christian Roman historian confirmed Jesus' crucifixion.**
