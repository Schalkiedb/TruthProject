# God's Special Sign: The Sabbath Rest
## A Comprehensive Bible Study Guide

---

## Introduction: The Sign Between God and His People

After studying the prophecies of Daniel and Revelation, after understanding who the little horn is, after seeing how the second beast enforces worship, after recognizing how evolution undermines Creation, and after discovering what the mark of the beast is—we now come to **God's answer**: His special sign, His holy Sabbath.

**This isn't just another doctrine**—it's the sign of the covenant between God and His people. It's the memorial of Creation, the test of obedience, the seal of God's law, and the promise of rest for those who trust Him.

**Foundation Principle**: The Sabbath is God's special sign—a mark of His creative power, His sanctifying grace, and His covenant relationship with His people.

**Study Methodology**: Let Scripture reveal when the Sabbath was established, who made it, why it matters, how it's observed, and what it means for God's end-time people.

## Part 1: The Repairer of the Breach

### The Call to Restore

**Isaiah 58:12**: "Those from among you shall build the old waste places; you shall raise up the foundations of many generations; and you shall be called **The repairer of the breach, The restorer of streets to dwell in**."

**God's end-time people will**:
- **Build the old waste places** (restore what has been torn down)
- **Raise up foundations of many generations** (return to ancient truths)
- Be called **"The repairer of the breach"** (fix what has been broken)
- Be called **"The restorer of streets to dwell in"** (make safe paths for others)

**What is the breach?** What has been broken that needs repair?

### The Breach Identified

**Isaiah 58:13-14**: "**If you turn away your foot from the Sabbath**, from doing your pleasure on **My holy day**, and call the Sabbath a delight, the holy day of the Lord honorable, and shall honor Him, not doing your own ways, nor finding your own pleasure, nor speaking your own words, **then you shall delight yourself in the Lord**; and I will cause you to ride on the high hills of the earth, and feed you with the heritage of Jacob your father. **The mouth of the Lord has spoken**."

**The breach = comprehensive covenant unfaithfulness**, including the oppression of workers (Isaiah 58:6-7: "loose the bonds of wickedness… let the oppressed go free") and **Sabbath desecration** (Isaiah 58:13). The immediate context (58:6-7) shows Isaiah first condemns exploitation and injustice; the Sabbath call in 58:13 is the capstone of full covenant restoration — right treatment of people AND right worship of God.

**God's call**:
1. **Turn away your foot from the Sabbath** (stop trampling it, stop ignoring it)
2. **Stop doing your pleasure** on His holy day
3. **Call the Sabbath a delight** (treasure it, enjoy it)
4. **Honor the holy day of the Lord** (treat it as sacred)
5. **Stop doing your own ways** (submit to His way)
6. **Stop seeking your own pleasure** (seek His will)
7. **Stop speaking your own words** (speak His truth)

**The promise**: "**Then you shall delight yourself in the Lord**"—intimacy with God comes through Sabbath-keeping.

**"The mouth of the Lord has spoken"**—This isn't optional. This is God's direct command.

## Part 2: Creation Week—When the Sabbath Began

### The Seventh Day Established

**Genesis 2:1-3**: "Thus the heavens and the earth were finished, and all the host of them. And on the seventh day **God ended His work which He had made**; and **He rested on the seventh day** from all His work which He had made. And **God blessed the seventh day, and sanctified it**: because that in it He had rested from all His work which God created and made."

**Three Actions God Took on the Seventh Day**:
1. **Rested** from His work (ceased from creating)
2. **Blessed** the seventh day (imparted special favor to it)
3. **Sanctified** it (set it apart as holy, made it sacred)

**Key point**: This happened **at Creation**, not at Sinai. The Sabbath was established before:
- Abraham was born
- Israel existed as a nation
- The law was given at Mount Sinai
- There were any Jews

**The Sabbath is not Jewish**—it's as old as Creation itself.

### God's Blessing is Permanent

**Malachi 3:6**: "**For I am the Lord, I do not change**; therefore you are not consumed, O sons of Jacob."

**Numbers 23:19**: "God is not a man, that He should lie, nor a son of man, that He should repent. **Has He said, and will He not do? Or has He spoken, and will He not make it good?**"

**When God blesses something**:
- It remains blessed **forever** — because God Himself does not change
- No human can reverse God's blessing
- The blessing continues across all generations

**God blessed the seventh day** (Genesis 2:3) → Therefore, the seventh day remains blessed forever. His unchanging nature (Malachi 3:6) guarantees what He has blessed stays blessed.

**No human authority has power to transfer God's blessing from the seventh day to the first day.**

**The Word** = Jesus Christ (revealed in verse 14)

**Jesus**:
- Was in the beginning with God
- Was God (deity, not created)
- Made **all things**
- Nothing exists that He didn't make

**John 1:14**: "And **the Word was made flesh**, and dwelt among us, (and we beheld His glory, the glory as of the only begotten of the Father,) full of grace and truth."

**The Word became flesh** = Jesus Christ, God incarnate

**Therefore**: If Jesus made **all things**, He made the Sabbath.

### Jesus Created All Things

**Ephesians 3:8-9**: "Unto me, who am less than the least of all saints, is this grace given, that I should preach among the Gentiles the unsearchable riches of Christ; And to make all men see what is the fellowship of the mystery, which from the beginning of the world hath been hid in God, **who created all things by Jesus Christ**."

**Clear statement**: God created all things **by Jesus Christ**.

**This includes**:
- The heavens and the earth (Genesis 1:1)
- The seventh-day Sabbath (Genesis 2:2-3)
- The blessing and sanctification of that day

**Jesus is the Creator of the Sabbath.**

### The Sabbath Was Made for Man

**Mark 2:27-28**: "And He said to them, '**The Sabbath was made for man, and not man for the Sabbath**. Therefore **the Son of Man is also Lord of the Sabbath**.'"

**Key truths**:

   - Not just for Jews
   - "Man" = humanity, all people

   - A gift, not a burden
   - For our benefit, not God's need

3. **"The Son of Man is Lord of the Sabbath"** (Jesus has authority over the Sabbath)
   - He made it
   - He defines it
   - He determines its meaning
   - No human authority supersedes His

**If Jesus is Lord of the Sabbath, only He can change it—and He never did.**

## Part 5: The Sabbath Before Sinai

### God Made Known His Sabbath

**Nehemiah 9:14**: "You **made known to them Your holy Sabbath**, and commanded them precepts, statutes and laws, by the hand of Moses Your servant."

**"Made known"** = revealed, declared, brought to their attention

This verse distinguishes between the Sabbath's *origin* and its formal *proclamation* at Sinai — God revealed ("made known") the Sabbath at Sinai, implying it was not created there. The **primary and definitive proof** that the Sabbath pre-existed Sinai is **Genesis 2:2-3** — God rested, blessed, and sanctified the seventh day at Creation, thousands of years before Moses or the nation of Israel. Nehemiah 9:14 complements this; the Exodus 16 manna miracle (below) confirms Israel was already expected to observe the Sabbath before Sinai.

### The Manna Test

**Exodus 16:4-5**: "Then the Lord said to Moses, 'Behold, I will rain bread from heaven for you. And the people shall go out and gather a certain quota every day, **that I may test them, whether they will walk in My law or not**. And it shall be on the sixth day that they shall prepare what they bring in, and it shall be twice as much as they gather daily.'"

**God's test**: Will they obey His law regarding the Sabbath?

**This happened before the Ten Commandments were given at Sinai** (Exodus 20).

**Exodus 16:23**: "Then he said to them, 'This is what the Lord has said: "Tomorrow is **a Sabbath rest, a holy Sabbath to the Lord**. Bake what you will bake today, and boil what you will boil; and lay up for yourselves all that remains, to be kept until morning."'"

**Before Sinai, God already called the seventh day**:
- A **Sabbath rest**
- A **holy Sabbath**
- **The Sabbath to the Lord** (belonging to God)

**Exodus 16:25-27**: "Then Moses said, 'Eat that today, for today is **a Sabbath to the Lord**; today you will not find it in the field. Six days you shall gather it, but **on the seventh day, which is the Sabbath, there will be none**.' Now it happened that some of the people went out on the seventh day to gather, but they found none."

**God enforced Sabbath-keeping with a miracle**:
- Double manna on the sixth day
- No manna on the seventh day
- Preserved manna on Sabbath (it didn't rot)
- Those who went out to gather on Sabbath found none

**This proves the Sabbath existed before Sinai and was already binding.**

## Part 6: The Sabbath Commandment

### A Sabbath of Solemn Rest

**Leviticus 23:3**: "**Six days shall work be done, but the seventh day is a Sabbath of solemn rest, a holy convocation**. You shall do no work on it; it is **the Sabbath of the Lord** in all your dwellings."

**Characteristics**:
- **Sabbath of solemn rest** (serious, sacred rest)
- **Holy convocation** (set apart for assembly/worship)
- **No work** (cessation from labor)
- **Sabbath of the Lord** (belongs to God, not man)
- **In all your dwellings** (everywhere, universally)

### When Does the Sabbath Begin?

**Leviticus 23:32**: "It shall be to you a Sabbath of solemn rest…**from evening to evening, you shall celebrate your Sabbath**."

**Sabbath time**: Evening to evening (sunset to sunset)

**Why?** Because that's how God counted days during Creation:

**Genesis 1:5**: "And God called the light Day, and the darkness he called Night. And **the evening and the morning were the first day**."

**Genesis 1:8**: "And God called the firmament Heaven. And **the evening and the morning were the second day**."

**Pattern**: Evening (night) comes first, then morning (day) = one complete day

**Therefore**: Sabbath begins at sunset Friday evening and ends at sunset Saturday evening.

## Part 7: Jesus Kept the Sabbath

### Jesus' Custom

**Luke 4:16**: "So He came to Nazareth, where He had been brought up. And **as His custom was, He went into the synagogue on the Sabbath day**, and stood up to read."

**"As His custom was"** = His regular habit, His consistent practice

**Jesus**:
- Kept the Sabbath faithfully
- Worshiped on the Sabbath
- Taught on the Sabbath
- Never changed the Sabbath

**Luke 4:31**: "Then He went down to Capernaum, a city of Galilee, and **was teaching them on the Sabbaths**."

**Jesus' teaching ministry was centered on Sabbath gatherings.**

### Even in Death, Jesus Honored the Sabbath

**Luke 23:55-56**: "And the women who had come with Him from Galilee followed after, and they observed the tomb and how His body was laid. Then they returned and prepared spices and fragrant oils. And **they rested on the Sabbath according to the commandment**."

**Jesus died Friday afternoon** (preparation day before Sabbath)

**The women**:
- Prepared spices Friday before sunset
- **Rested on the Sabbath** (Saturday)
- Came to the tomb Sunday morning (first day of the week)

**Luke 24:1**: "Now **on the first day of the week**, very early in the morning, they, and certain other women with them, came to the tomb bringing the spices which they had prepared."

**Mark 16:1-2**: "Now **when the Sabbath was past**, Mary Magdalene, Mary the mother of James, and Salome bought spices, that they might come and anoint Him. Very early in the morning, **on the first day of the week**, they came to the tomb when the sun had risen."

**Clear timeline**:
- **Friday** (preparation day): Jesus crucified, buried before sunset
- **Saturday** (Sabbath): Women rested "according to the commandment"
- **Sunday** (first day): Women came to tomb, found Jesus risen

**Even Jesus' followers kept the Sabbath after His death**—they didn't switch to Sunday.

**Even in death, Jesus rested in the tomb on the Sabbath**, honoring the commandment He had made at Creation.

## Part 8: The Apostles Kept the Sabbath

### Paul's Custom

**Acts 13:14**: "But when they departed from Perga, they came to Antioch in Pisidia, and **went into the synagogue on the Sabbath day** and sat down."

**Acts 17:2**: "Then Paul, **as his custom was**, went in to them, and **for three Sabbaths reasoned with them from the Scriptures**."

**"As his custom was"** = Paul's regular practice (same phrase used of Jesus in Luke 4:16)

**Paul kept the Sabbath** just as Jesus did.

### Preaching to Gentiles on the Sabbath

**Acts 13:42-44**: "So when the Jews went out of the synagogue, **the Gentiles begged that these words might be preached to them the next Sabbath**. Now when the congregation had broken up, many of the Jews and devout proselytes followed Paul and Barnabas, who, speaking to them, persuaded them to continue in the grace of God. **On the next Sabbath almost the whole city came together to hear the word of God**."

**Critical point**: The Gentiles asked Paul to preach **"the next Sabbath"**—not Sunday.

**If Paul taught that the Sabbath was abolished or changed to Sunday, this was the perfect opportunity to tell them**. Instead, he waited until the next Sabbath and "almost the whole city" gathered to hear the Word of God.

**Acts 16:12-13**: "and from there to Philippi, which is the foremost city of that part of Macedonia, a colony. And we were staying in that city for some days. And **on the Sabbath day we went out of the city to the riverside, where prayer was customarily made**; and we sat down and spoke to the women who met there."

**Philippi** = Gentile city with few Jews

**Paul still kept the Sabbath** even in Gentile territories.

**Acts 18:4**: "And he reasoned in the synagogue **every Sabbath**, and persuaded both **Jews and Greeks**."

**Paul preached to both Jews and Greeks (Gentiles) on the Sabbath—not on Sunday.**

## Part 9: The Sabbath Rest Remains

### Entering God's Rest

**Psalm 95:6-11**: "O come, let us worship and bow down: let us kneel before the LORD our maker. For He is our God; and we are the people of His pasture, and the sheep of His hand. **To day if ye will hear His voice, harden not your heart**, as in the provocation, and as in the day of temptation in the wilderness: When your fathers tempted Me, proved Me, and saw My work. Forty years long was I grieved with this generation, and said, It is a people that do err in their heart, and they have not known My ways: Unto whom I sware in my wrath **that they should not enter into my rest**."

**God's rest** = both physical (Sabbath rest) and spiritual (salvation rest through faith)

**Israel in the wilderness**:
- Hardened their hearts
- Tempted God
- Failed to trust Him
- Did not enter His rest (died in wilderness)

### Hebrews Explains the Rest

**Hebrews 4:3-4**: "For we which have believed do enter into rest, as He said, As I have sworn in My wrath, if they shall enter into My rest: although the works were finished from the foundation of the world. For **He spake in a certain place of the seventh day on this wise, And God did rest the seventh day from all His works**."

**The rest** refers back to **Genesis 2:2-3**—the seventh-day Sabbath rest established at Creation.

**Hebrews 4:5-7**: "And in this place again, If they shall enter into My rest. Seeing therefore it remaineth that some must enter therein, and they to whom it was first preached entered not in because of unbelief: Again, He limiteth a certain day, saying in David, **To day**, after so long a time; as it is said, **To day if ye will hear His voice, harden not your hearts**."

**"It remaineth"** = The rest is still available, still future

**Hebrews 4:8-9**: "For if Jesus had given them rest, then would He not afterward have spoken of another day. **There remaineth therefore a rest to the people of God**."

**Greek word for "rest" in verse 9**: *sabbatismos* = **Sabbath rest**, a term found only here in the New Testament, meaning "a keeping of the Sabbath" or "Sabbath observance."

**The Adventist interpretation** (consistent with the historic Sabbatarian position): *sabbatismos* refers to the literal weekly Sabbath — the text explicitly ties the rest back to "the seventh day" (verse 4, quoting Genesis 2:2) and distinguishes it from the rest Joshua gave (verse 8). Since a distinct "Sabbath-keeping" still remains, the seventh-day Sabbath continues for God's people.

*(Note: Some interpreters read Hebrews 4 as pointing primarily to the eschatological rest of salvation — entering God's salvation rest through faith in Christ. The Adventist reading holds both dimensions together: literal Sabbath rest is the weekly sign of the salvation rest believers already enjoy. The key point is that the Sabbath rest is not abolished — it "remains.")*

**This was written decades after the cross**—the Sabbath rest still remains for God's people.

**Hebrews 4:10-11**: "For he that is entered into His rest, he also hath ceased from his own works, as God did from His. **Let us labour therefore to enter into that rest**, lest any man fall after the same example of unbelief."

**Parallel**:
- **God rested** from His works (Genesis 2:2)
- **We rest** from our works (cease from self-effort for salvation, trust in Christ's finished work)
- **Physical Sabbath rest** = sign of **spiritual rest in Christ**

**Warning**: Don't fall into unbelief like Israel did—enter His rest (both salvation and Sabbath).

## Part 10: The Lord's Day

### Which Day Is the Lord's Day?

**Revelation 1:10**: "**I was in the Spirit on the Lord's Day**, and I heard behind me a loud voice, as of a trumpet."

**Many claim "the Lord's Day" = Sunday.** Post-apostolic writers (Ignatius, c. 110 AD; the *Didache*; Barnabas) did use the Greek *kyriakē hēmera* ("the Lord's Day") to refer to Sunday. However, Revelation 1:10 itself does not define which day is meant — the definition must come from Scripture.

**The Adventist interpretation** (grounded in the Old Testament foundation): "the Lord's Day" = the seventh-day Sabbath, on the basis that:

**Isaiah 58:13**: "If you turn away your foot from the Sabbath, from doing your pleasure on **My holy day**… **the holy day of the Lord**…"

**God calls the Sabbath**:
- **"My holy day"**
- **"The holy day of the Lord"**

**The consistent biblical pattern** is that *the Lord* claims the seventh-day Sabbath as His own — not the first day. When John says he was "in the Spirit on the Lord's Day," the biblical definition of which day belongs to the Lord points to the Sabbath.

### Ezekiel Confirms

**Ezekiel 20:12**: "Moreover I also gave them **My Sabbaths**, to be a sign between them and Me, that they might know that I am the Lord who sanctifies them."

**Ezekiel 20:20**: "hallow **My Sabbaths**, and they will be a sign between Me and you, that you may know that I am the Lord your God."

**"My Sabbaths"** = The Sabbath belongs to the Lord.

**The Lord's Day is not Sunday—it's the seventh-day Sabbath that God blessed, sanctified, and claimed as His own.**

## Part 11: Worship in Spirit and Truth

### The Samaritan Woman's Question

**John 4:20-24**: "'Our fathers worshiped on this mountain, and you Jews say that in Jerusalem is the place where one ought to worship.' Jesus said to her, 'Woman, believe Me, the hour is coming when you will neither on this mountain, nor in Jerusalem, worship the Father. You worship what you do not know; we know what we worship, for salvation is of the Jews. But the hour is coming, and now is, when **the true worshipers will worship the Father in spirit and truth**; for the Father is seeking such to worship Him. **God is Spirit, and those who worship Him must worship in spirit and truth**.'"

**The question**: WHERE should we worship? (Location)

**Jesus' answer**: 
- Not about WHERE (mountain vs. Jerusalem)
- About HOW: **in spirit and truth**

**"In spirit"** = with sincere heart, genuine devotion, Holy Spirit leading
**"In truth"** = according to God's Word, not human tradition

**True worship requires BOTH**:
1. **Spirit** (right attitude, sincere heart)
2. **Truth** (right doctrine, biblical accuracy)

**You cannot worship in "spirit" while ignoring "truth"**—sincerity doesn't override obedience to God's Word.

### Worship the Creator

**Psalm 100:2-3**: "Serve the Lord with gladness; Come before His presence with singing. **Know that the Lord, He is God; It is He who has made us**, and not we ourselves; we are His people and the sheep of His pasture."

**Worship is based on Creation**: "It is He who has made us"

**The Sabbath is the memorial of Creation** (Exodus 20:11)—therefore, the Sabbath is central to worship.

**John 10:27-28**: "**My sheep hear My voice, and I know them, and they follow Me**. And I give them eternal life, and they shall never perish; neither shall anyone snatch them out of My hand."

**Jesus' sheep**:
- **Hear His voice** (listen to His Word, not human tradition)
- **Follow Him** (obey His commandments, including the Sabbath)

**If we are His sheep, we follow what He says—not what man has changed.**

## Part 12: The Sabbath—God's Sign

### A Sign Between God and His People

**Exodus 31:13**: "Speak also to the children of Israel, saying: '**Surely My Sabbaths you shall keep, for it is a sign between Me and you throughout your generations**, that you may know that I am the Lord who sanctifies you."

**Important context**: Exodus 31 addresses "the children of Israel" specifically — this is the Mosaic covenant administration of the Sabbath given to the nation of Israel. However, the Sabbath's *origin* is not Sinai but **Genesis 2:2-3** — established at Creation before Israel existed, before Abraham, before any distinction between Jew and Gentile. The Sabbath was given to "man" (Mark 2:27), not to Israel alone.

**The Sabbath is a SIGN**:
- Between God and His people
- Rooted in Creation (Genesis 2:2-3), formalized at Sinai for Israel, and applicable to all who belong to God
- That God sanctifies (makes holy) His people

**Exodus 31:17**: "**It is a sign between Me and the children of Israel forever**; for in six days the Lord made the heavens and the earth, and on the seventh day He rested and was refreshed."

**"Forever"** = for all time, perpetually, without end. The sign's permanence is rooted in Creation itself — "*for* in six days the Lord made the heavens and the earth" — not in the temporary Mosaic economy.

**The sign points to**:
- God as Creator ("in six days the Lord made the heavens and the earth")
- God's rest on the seventh day
- God's covenant relationship with His people

**Who are "the children of Israel"?**

In the New Testament, **all believers in Jesus are spiritual Israel**:

**Romans 2:28-29**: "For he is not a Jew who is one outwardly, nor is circumcision that which is outward in the flesh; but he is a Jew who is one inwardly; and circumcision is that of the heart, in the Spirit, not in the letter; whose praise is not from men but from God."

**Galatians 3:28-29**: "There is neither Jew nor Greek, there is neither slave nor free, there is neither male nor female; for you are all one in Christ Jesus. And if you are Christ's, then you are Abraham's seed, and heirs according to the promise."

**If you belong to Christ, you are Abraham's seed—spiritual Israel—and the Sabbath sign applies to you.**

## Part 13: Sign and Seal

### Understanding Biblical Signs and Seals

**Exodus 31:13** (repeated for emphasis): "Speak also to the children of Israel, saying: 'Surely My Sabbaths you shall keep, **for it is a sign between Me and you throughout your generations**, that you may know that I am the Lord who sanctifies you."

**A SIGN**:
- Identifies ownership
- Marks relationship
- Distinguishes God's people from the world

**Romans 4:11**: "And he received **the sign of circumcision, a seal of the righteousness of the faith** which he had while still uncircumcised…"

**Notice**: "sign" and "seal" are connected—a sign can function as a seal. Romans 4:11 establishes that an outward sign can serve as a seal of an inward spiritual reality (Abraham's faith).

**Circumcision's New Covenant counterpart** is baptism (Colossians 2:11-12: "circumcised with the circumcision made without hands… buried with Him in baptism"). **The Sabbath stands on a different and deeper foundation** — it is not a replacement for circumcision but a Creation-grounded sign established at Genesis 2:2-3, confirmed as God's ongoing covenant sign in Exodus 31:13 and Ezekiel 20:12, and rooted in God's own rest.

**The Sabbath as sign**:
- Established at Creation (before circumcision or any covenant ceremony)
- Belongs to the Lord ("My Sabbaths" — Ezekiel 20:12, 20)
- Marks those who know God as Creator and Sanctifier

**2 Timothy 2:19**: "Nevertheless **the solid foundation of God stands, having this seal**: '**The Lord knows those who are His**,' and, 'Let everyone who names the name of Christ **depart from iniquity**.'"

**God's seal identifies**:
1. Those whom **the Lord knows** (relationship)
2. Those who **depart from iniquity** (obedience)

**Matthew 7:23**: "And then will I [Jesus] profess unto them, **I never knew you**: depart from me, ye that work iniquity."

**Those without God's seal**:
- Jesus doesn't know them
- They work iniquity (lawlessness, disobedience to God's law)

**The Sabbath is the sign that God knows us and we know Him—it identifies us as His sanctified people.**

## Part 14: Moses Chose the Sabbath

### Refusing Egypt's Pleasures

**Hebrews 11:24-26**: "By faith Moses, when he became of age, **refused to be called the son of Pharaoh's daughter**, choosing rather **to suffer affliction with the people of God than to enjoy the passing pleasures of sin**, esteeming the reproach of Christ greater riches than the treasures in Egypt; for he looked to the reward."

**Moses' choice**:
- **Refused** Egypt's wealth, power, and status
- **Chose** affliction with God's people
- **Esteemed** Christ's reproach greater than Egypt's treasures
- **Looked** to eternal reward

**What was one of the "pleasures of sin" Moses refused?**

**Exodus 5:5**: "And Pharaoh said, 'Look, the people of the land are many now, and **you make them rest from their labor**!'"

**Pharaoh's complaint** was that Moses was disrupting the labor force by causing the people to rest. While this may allude to Sabbath-keeping (consistent with Exodus 16 showing the Sabbath was observed before Sinai), the text does not specify the seventh-day Sabbath explicitly — Pharaoh's language is broader, referring to any rest from work. The connection is reasonable but should be read as suggestive rather than definitive proof.

**The broader spiritual lesson is clear**: in Egypt, any act of covenant loyalty to God — including Sabbath rest — came at a cost:
- Economic disadvantage (lost labor)
- Ridicule and opposition from the surrounding culture
- Persecution from those in power
- Seeming foolishness to the world

**Moses chose affliction with God's people over Egypt's passing pleasures—this is the faith needed for the final crisis.**

## Part 15: The Sabbath and Salvation

### Remember Your Deliverance

**Deuteronomy 5:12-15**: "**Keep the Sabbath day to sanctify it**, as the LORD thy God hath commanded thee. Six days thou shalt labour, and do all thy work: But the seventh day is the Sabbath of the LORD thy God: in it thou shalt not do any work… And **remember that thou wast a servant in the land of Egypt, and that the LORD thy God brought thee out thence** through a mighty hand and by a stretched out arm: therefore the LORD thy God commanded thee to keep the Sabbath day."

**Two reasons for Sabbath-keeping**:
1. **Creation** (Exodus 20:11)—God made us
2. **Redemption** (Deuteronomy 5:15)—God saved us

**The Sabbath reminds us**:
- God created us (we belong to Him by creation)
- God delivered us (we belong to Him by redemption)

**Egypt** = slavery, bondage to sin
**Deliverance** = salvation through God's mighty power

**Physical deliverance from Egypt** = picture of **spiritual deliverance from sin**

### Jesus Saves from Sin

**Matthew 1:21**: "…thou shalt call His name **JESUS: for He shall save His people from their sins**."

**Jesus** = "Yahweh saves" or "Savior"

**He saves us FROM sin, not IN sin**—salvation includes deliverance from sin's power, not just its penalty.

**The Sabbath is a weekly reminder**:
- God created us
- God redeemed us
- We rest from our own works (cease trying to save ourselves)
- We trust in His finished work (both Creation and Calvary)

**Matthew 12:8**: "For the Son of man is **Lord even of the Sabbath day**."

**Jesus is Lord of the Sabbath** because:
- He created it
- He kept it
- He is the rest it points to
- He delivers us (the ultimate deliverance the Sabbath commemorates)

## Part 16: The Sabbath for All Nations

### The Foreigner and the Sabbath

## Part 17: The Sabbath Commandment

### Remember the Sabbath Day

**Exodus 20:8-11**: "**Remember the Sabbath day, to keep it holy**. Six days you shall labor and do all your work, but **the seventh day is the Sabbath of the Lord your God**. In it you shall do no work: you, nor your son, nor your daughter, nor your male servant, nor your female servant, nor your cattle, nor your stranger who is within your gates. **For in six days the Lord made the heavens and the earth, the sea, and all that is in them, and rested the seventh day. Therefore the Lord blessed the Sabbath day and hallowed it**."

**The Fourth Commandment**:

1. **"Remember"** (don't forget, recall, call to mind)
2. **"The Sabbath day"** (the specific day, not just any day)
3. **"To keep it holy"** (set apart, treat as sacred)
4. **"The seventh day"** (not the first day, not just "one day in seven")
5. **"Is the Sabbath of the Lord your God"** (belongs to God, not man)
6. **"In it you shall do no work"** (cessation from labor)
7. **Universal application**: you, your family, your servants, your animals, foreigners—everyone
8. **Reason**: Creation—God made everything in six days and rested the seventh day
9. **Result**: God blessed and hallowed (made holy) the Sabbath day

**This is not a suggestion—it's a commandment from the Ten Commandments, the moral law of God.**

## Part 18: The Seal in God's Law

### Understanding Ancient Seals

**1 Kings 21:7-8**: "And Jezebel his wife said unto him, Dost thou now govern the kingdom of Israel?… So **she wrote letters in Ahab's name, and sealed them with his seal**…"

**A seal in ancient times**:
- Authenticated a document
- Proved authority
- Showed ownership
- Could not be reversed once sealed

**Esther 8:8**: "…seal it with the king's ring: **for the writing which is written in the king's name, and sealed with the king's ring, may no man reverse**."

**A seal contains three elements**:
1. **Name** (who)
2. **Title** (what authority/position)
3. **Territory** (where/what jurisdiction)

**Example—U.S. Presidential seal**:
- Name: [President's name]
- Title: President
- Territory: United States of America

### The Sabbath Contains All Three Elements

**Exodus 20:8-11** (Fourth Commandment):

**Element 1—NAME**: "the **LORD** your God" (Yahweh, Jehovah, the covenant name of God)

**Element 2—TITLE**: "**made**" (Creator, Maker—His authoritative position)

**Element 3—TERRITORY**: "**the heavens and the earth, the sea, and all that is in them**" (His jurisdiction—everything)

**Only the Sabbath commandment contains all three elements of a seal.**

**The other nine commandments**:
- Don't contain God's name, title, and territory together
- Are principles of conduct
- Are authenticated by the Sabbath commandment (the seal in the law)

**This is why the Sabbath is called God's SIGN and SEAL** (Exodus 31:13, 17; Ezekiel 20:12, 20)—it authenticates the entire law.
