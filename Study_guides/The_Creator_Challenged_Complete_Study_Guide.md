## Part 1: The Three Angels' Messages - Overview

## Part 9: Scientific Admissions - Evolution's Failures

*The following citations come from scientists working within the evolutionary framework who nonetheless made candid admissions about its unresolved problems. These are not creationist quotes — they are honest acknowledgments from within the scientific community itself. In each case, the full source context is noted.*

### No Evidence for Transmutation of Species

**Thomas Hunt Morgan** (Geneticist, Columbia & Caltech):
> "**Within the period of human history we do not know of a single instance of the transmutation of one species into another one**... This must be admitted."

**Fact**: No observed instance of one species becoming another species.

**Austin Clark** (Biologist, Smithsonian Institution):
> "**Since we have not the slightest evidence, either among the living or the fossil animals, of intergrading types following the major groups, it is a fair supposition that there never have been any**."

**Fact**: No transitional forms in living organisms or fossils between major groups.

### Genetics Contradicts Evolution

**George W. Burns** (Geneticist):
> "**Recombination... merely redistributes existing genetic material among different individuals; it makes no change in it**."

**Fact**: Genetic recombination (breeding, natural selection) only shuffles existing information—it doesn't create new genetic information.

**Richard B. Goldschmidt** (Biologist, U.C. Berkeley):
> "**Microevolution by accumulation of micro-mutations... leads to diversification strictly within the species**... The step from one species to another requires another evolutionary method than that of sheer accumulation of micro-mutations."

**Fact**: 
- **Microevolution** (variation within species) = observable, happens ✓
- **Macroevolution** (one species to another) = not observable, requires different mechanism that has never been demonstrated ✗

### The Honest Admission

**George Wald** (Biology, Harvard—Nobel Prize winner):
> "**One has only to contemplate the magnitude of this task to concede that the spontaneous generation of a living organism is impossible. Yet here we are—as a result, I believe, of spontaneous generation**."

**Translation**: 
1. Admits spontaneous generation (life from non-life) is **impossible**
2. Yet believes it happened anyway
3. **Why?** Because the only alternative is a Creator—which he rejects

**This is not science—it's philosophy, it's faith in materialism despite the evidence.**
