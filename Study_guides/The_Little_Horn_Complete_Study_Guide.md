## A Comprehensive Bible Study Guide

## Introduction: The Warning Against Deception

Before Christ's return, the apostle Paul warned believers about a great deception that would arise. This isn't a peripheral issue—it's central to understanding end-time prophecy and avoiding the snares set for God's people. The Little Horn power represents one of the most detailed prophecies in Scripture, given multiple times with increasing clarity. This study will let the Bible identify this power using its own definitions and prophetic principles.

**Foundation Principle**: "Let no one deceive you by any means" (2 Thessalonians 2:3). God has given us clear identifying marks so we won't be fooled.

**Study Methodology**: We will use Scripture to interpret Scripture, letting the Bible define its own symbols. Every identifying characteristic comes directly from God's Word, not human speculation.

**Bible Versions**: This guide primarily uses the King James Version (KJV). Where the New King James Version (NKJV) is quoted, it is noted in context. Both versions are used only where they best preserve the literal sense of the original text.

## Part 1: The Man of Sin Revealed

### The Prophecy of Lawlessness

**2 Thessalonians 2:1-4** provides Paul's warning about events that must occur before Christ's return:

**Verse 1-2**: Paul addresses confusion about Christ's coming and our gathering to Him. Believers were being "shaken in mind or troubled" by false teachings claiming "the day of Christ had come."

**Verse 3**: "Let **no man** deceive you by any means; for that day shall not come, except there come **a falling away first**, and **that man of sin be revealed, the son of perdition**." (KJV)

**Two Prerequisites for Christ's Return**:
1. **The falling away** (apostasia in Greek = apostasy, rebellion against truth)
2. **The man of sin revealed** (a specific power opposing God)

**Verse 4**: This power "opposes and exalts himself **above all that is called God** or that is worshiped, so that **he sits as God in the temple of God, showing himself that he is God**."

### Key Identifying Marks from 2 Thessalonians 2:
- **Claims divine authority** ("sits as God")
- **Operates within religious context** ("temple of God")
- **Exalts himself above God's authority**
- **Arose after apostolic restraint removed** (verses 6-7)
- **Operates with satanic power** (verse 9: "according to the working of Satan")
- **Uses deception and false miracles** (verses 9-10)

### The Restrainer Removed

**2 Thessalonians 2:6-7**: "And now you know what is restraining, that he may be revealed in his own time. For the mystery of lawlessness is already at work; **only he who now restrains will do so until he is taken out of the way**."

**Historical Context**: The restraining power was the Roman Empire. While pagan Rome ruled, the papal system could not rise to full power. Once the Western Roman Empire fell (476 CE) and the last opposing tribes were removed (538 CE), the "mystery of lawlessness" was fully revealed.

**2 Thessalonians 2:8**: "And then the lawless one will be revealed, whom the Lord will consume with the breath of His mouth and **destroy with the brightness of His coming**."

**Critical Point**: This power operates **until Christ's return**. It's not an ancient, extinct system—it remains active until the Second Coming.

## Part 2: Christ's Warning About False Claims

### Jesus' Prediction of Deception

**Matthew 24:5**: "For **many shall come in My name**, saying, I am Christ; and shall deceive many."

**Note the precision**: They don't deny Christ. They come **"in My name"** (claiming to represent Christ), saying **"I am Christ"** (claiming Christ's authority and position).

**Matthew 24:24**: "For there shall arise **false Christs, and false prophets**, and shall show **great signs and wonders**; insomuch that, **if it were possible, they shall deceive the very elect**."

**The Depth of Deception**:
- So convincing it would deceive **even the elect** (if possible)
- Accompanied by **miraculous signs** (not obviously evil)
- Multiple false systems (**many**, not just one)
- Operating under Christ's name (appearing Christian)

### The Satanic Source

**2 Thessalonians 2:9-10**: "The coming of the lawless one is **according to the working of Satan**, with all power, signs, and lying wonders, and with all unrighteous deception among those who perish, because **they did not receive the love of the truth**, that they might be saved."

**Satan's Strategy**:
- **Supernatural power** (mimicking God's miracles)
- **"Lying wonders"** (genuine supernatural events serving false theology)
- **Unrighteous deception** (looks righteous, feels spiritual, but leads away from truth)
- **Targets those without love for truth** (emotional experience over biblical accuracy)

### The Heart's Deception

**Jeremiah 17:9**: "The heart is **deceitful above all things, and desperately wicked**; who can know it?"

**Application**: We cannot trust feelings, traditions, or majority opinion. Only Scripture can reliably identify truth from error.

## Part 8: The Prophetic Time Period

### Calculating 1,260 Years

Three different prophetic descriptions refer to the **same time period**:

**Daniel 7:25**: "...and they shall be given into his hand until a **time and times and the dividing of time**."

**Biblical Year-Day Principle**:
- **Time** = 1 prophetic year = 360 days *(cross-reference: Revelation 11:2-3 gives 42 months and 1,260 days for the same period — 1,260 ÷ 42 = 30 days/month × 12 months = 360-day prophetic year)*
- **Times** = 2 prophetic years = 720 days  
- **Dividing of time** = ½ prophetic year = 180 days
- **Total**: 360 + 720 + 180 = **1,260 prophetic days**

**Ezekiel 4:6**: "I have appointed thee **each day for a year**."

**Numbers 14:34**: "After the number of the days in which ye searched the land, even forty days, **each day for a year**..."

**Prophetic Principle Established**: In symbolic prophecy, one day = one literal year.

**Therefore**: 1,260 prophetic days = **1,260 literal years**

**Revelation 13:5**: "...and power was given unto him to continue **forty and two months**."

**Calculation**: 
- 42 months × 30 days/month = **1,260 days** = **1,260 years**

**Revelation 11:3**: "And I will give power unto My two witnesses, and they shall prophesy a **thousand two hundred and threescore days** [1260], clothed in sackcloth."

**All Three Descriptions = 1,260 Years of Supremacy**

This is the period during which the Little Horn power would:
- Persecute the saints
- Speak blasphemies against God
- Attempt to change God's law
- Rule with combined religious and political authority

## Part 13: The Number of His Name

### Historical Title: Vicarius Filii Dei

**The common papal title** today is "Vicar of Christ" (*Vicarius Christi*). The specific Latin form used in gematria is **"Vicar of the Son of God"** = **VICARIUS FILII DEI**.

This extended form appears in the *Donation of Constantine* (an 8th-century document long used to support papal temporal power) and in early papal historical records. The *Catholic Encyclopedia* (1913, Vol. XV, p. 403) acknowledges the title. While "Vicarius Filii Dei" is not the papacy's primary formal title today, it has been historically applied to the Pope in official documents and Catholic literature.

**What the papacy officially claims** about the role the title describes:
> *"The Pope is not only the representative of Jesus Christ, but he is Jesus Christ Himself, hidden under the veil of flesh."*
> — *The Catholic National*, July 1895

> The Pope is considered **"the man on earth who takes the place of the Son of God."**
> — *Prompta Bibliotheca*, Ferraris, Vol. VI, "Papa," Art. 2

**"Vicar of the Son of God"** in Latin = **VICARIUS FILII DEI**

**Latin Numerical Values**:
In Latin (like Hebrew and Greek), letters have numerical values. Note: In classical Latin, **U and V are the same letter** — both written as V and both carry the value of 5:

**VICARIUS** = V(5) + I(1) + C(100) + A(0) + R(0) + I(1) + V/U(5) + S(0) = **112**

**FILII** = F(0) + I(1) + L(50) + I(1) + I(1) = **53**

**DEI** = D(500) + E(0) + I(1) = **501**

**TOTAL**: 112 + 53 + 501 = **666**

**Significance**: "Vicar" means "substitute" or "in place of." Claiming to be "Vicar of the Son of God" = claiming Christ's position on earth = blasphemy (making oneself equal with God).

**Note**: This is one application. The number may have multiple fulfillments, but this historical title precisely matches the biblical calculation and the prophetic description of a power claiming to stand in Christ's place.

**Once removed** = papal power rises to full authority

**538 CE** = official beginning of the 1,260-year period of supremacy
